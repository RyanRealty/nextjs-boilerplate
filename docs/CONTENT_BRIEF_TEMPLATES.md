# Ryan Realty — Content Brief Templates by Page Type

Use these so copy and SEO content can be developed in parallel with the build. Fill in the bracketed parts per page; keep tone premium, accurate, and oriented to Central Oregon (or the target market) buyers and sellers.

---

## 1. Listing Detail Page

**Purpose:** Maximum SEO and maximum usefulness. Convert browsers into leads; rank for address and area + “real estate” queries.

**URL pattern:** `/real-estate/oregon/bend/[neighborhood/]community/address-city-state-zip/`

**Metadata**
- **Title:** `[Full address or “MLS# X”] | [City], [State] Real Estate | Ryan Realty`
- **Meta description:** 1–2 sentences: [Price], [beds/baths/sqft], [notable feature — e.g. “golf course frontage”]. [One differentiator]. In [Community], [City]. [Soft CTA, e.g. “View details and schedule a showing.”] (≤155 chars.)
- **OG/Twitter:** Same headline + listing hero image. Description = meta description.

**Structured data:** RealEstateListing (price, address, beds, baths, sqft, lot, listing agent, photos, video if any). BreadcrumbList. LocalBusiness for Ryan Realty.

**On-page content**
- **Hero:** Address (H1 in context), price, key stats. Gallery and video above the fold.
- **Description:** 2–4 short paragraphs. Lead with location (community, neighborhood, city). Include lifestyle fit (e.g. golf, schools, commute). Use natural keyword phrases (“homes in Tetherow,” “Bend Oregon real estate”). No keyword stuffing.
- **Details:** Every SPARK field that has a value, in collapsible sections. Empty sections not shown.
- **Internal links:** Community page, neighborhood page (if any), city page, “Similar homes in [Community],” market report for area.

**Content brief for copywriter**
- [ ] Address and MLS#
- [ ] List price and any price history note
- [ ] Beds, baths, sqft, lot size, year built
- [ ] Community and neighborhood (if any)
- [ ] 2–3 unique selling points (view, lot, finishes, location)
- [ ] Target buyer (e.g. “golf community buyer,” “family near schools”)
- [ ] Geographic keywords to weave in (community name, city, “Central Oregon”)
- [ ] CTA: “Schedule a showing” / “Get more info”

## 2. Community Page — Type One (Standard Subdivision)

**Purpose:** Rank for “[Community name] real estate” and “[Community name] homes for sale.” Clean, factual, no fluff.

**URL pattern:** `/real-estate/oregon/bend/[neighborhood/]community-slug/`

**Metadata**
- **Title:** `[Community Name] Real Estate | Homes for Sale in [City] | Ryan Realty`
- **Meta description:** [Community] in [City], [State]: [one-line descriptor]. [X] homes for sale. [One fact — e.g. price range or “family-friendly”]. View listings and market info. (≤155 chars.)

**Structured data:** Residence or Place, ItemList (listings), BreadcrumbList. LocalBusiness.

**On-page content**
- **Hero:** Community name, tagline or one-line descriptor. Optional single strong image (no resort-style hero).
- **Intro:** 1–2 paragraphs. Where it is, who it’s for, one or two concrete differentiators. No invented amenities.
- **Listings:** Current for-sale listings in this community (grid + map).
- **Market snapshot:** If data exists — e.g. median price, DOM, count. Link to full market report.
- **Internal links:** City page, neighborhood (if any), “All [City] communities,” relevant market report.

**Content brief**
- [ ] Official community name and any alternate spellings
- [ ] City and neighborhood (if assigned)
- [ ] Short factual description (boundaries, character, typical buyer)
- [ ] Any verifiable facts (e.g. number of homes, HOA name)
- [ ] Keywords: “[Community] real estate,” “[Community] homes for sale,” “[City] subdivision”

## 3. Community Page — Type Two (Resort / Amenity-Rich)

**Purpose:** Outrank the resort’s own site on real estate queries. Microsite: immersive, shareable, conversion-focused.

**URL pattern:** `/real-estate/oregon/bend/[neighborhood/]community-slug/`

**Metadata**
- **Title:** `[Community Name] Real Estate | Luxury [Golf/Resort] Homes in [City] | Ryan Realty`
- **Meta description:** [Community]: [one compelling line]. [Golf/resort/amenity]. [Price or lifestyle hook]. Current listings and market data. The authority on [Community] real estate. (≤155 chars.)

**Structured data:** Resort, GolfCourse (if applicable), AmenityFeature, ItemList (listings), BreadcrumbList, FAQPage, LocalBusiness.

**On-page content**
- **Hero:** Immersive video or high-impact imagery. Headline + one-line value prop. CTA: “View listings” / “Request a guide.”
- **Overview:** 2–3 paragraphs. Lifestyle, amenities, membership (if relevant). Why buyers choose it. Geographic and keyword context.
- **Amenities:** Clear list (golf, club, pool, trails, etc.). Schema: AmenityFeature.
- **Listings:** Current for-sale. Grid + map. “View all [Community] listings.”
- **Market data:** Current stats; link to full report.
- **Lifestyle / editorial:** Optional 1–2 sections (e.g. “Living at [Community],” “Seasonal highlights”). Shareable, quotable.
- **FAQ:** 5–10 questions (e.g. “What is the price range in [Community]?” “Is there an HOA?”). FAQPage schema.
- **Internal links:** City, neighborhood (if any), “Luxury communities in [City],” market report, featured listings.

**Content brief**
- [ ] Official name, positioning (“luxury golf community,” “resort community”)
- [ ] Amenities list (verified)
- [ ] Membership or HOA basics (what’s public vs member)
- [ ] Typical price range and buyer
- [ ] 2–3 lifestyle angles for editorial
- [ ] FAQ list (prices, HOA, golf, schools, access)
- [ ] Keywords: “[Community] real estate,” “luxury homes [Community],” “[City] golf community,” “Central Oregon resort real estate”

## 4. City Page

**Purpose:** Rank for “[City] real estate,” “[City] homes for sale,” “homes for sale in [City] Oregon.”

**URL pattern:** `/real-estate/oregon/city-slug/`

**Metadata**
- **Title:** `[City] Real Estate | Homes for Sale in [City], Oregon | Ryan Realty`
- **Meta description:** [City] Oregon real estate: [one-line hook]. [X] homes for sale. Browse by community and neighborhood. Market reports and local expertise. (≤155 chars.)

**Structured data:** Place (city), ItemList or aggregateOffer for listings, BreadcrumbList. LocalBusiness.

**On-page content**
- **Hero:** City name + tagline. Strong image or video.
- **Intro:** 1–2 paragraphs. Why [City]: lifestyle, location, market in one sentence. Geographic context.
- **Browse:** Links to neighborhoods (if any) and communities. Listings grid + map.
- **Market snapshot:** Link to city market report.
- **Internal links:** State/region, communities, market reports, “Contact us for [City].”

**Content brief**
- [ ] City name and state
- [ ] One-line positioning (e.g. “Gateway to the Cascades,” “Golf and outdoor lifestyle”)
- [ ] 2–3 bullet differentiators
- [ ] Keywords: “[City] real estate,” “homes for sale [City] Oregon,” “Central Oregon [City]”

## 5. Neighborhood Page

**Purpose:** Rank for “[Neighborhood] real estate,” “[Neighborhood] [City] homes.” Manually created; no SPARK source.

**URL pattern:** `/real-estate/oregon/bend/neighborhood-slug/`

**Metadata**
- **Title:** `[Neighborhood] Real Estate | [City] Oregon Homes & Communities | Ryan Realty`
- **Meta description:** [Neighborhood] in [City]: [one-line descriptor]. Communities and homes for sale. [One fact]. Browse listings and market data. (≤155 chars.)

**Structured data:** Place, ItemList (listings in neighborhood via communities), BreadcrumbList.

**On-page content**
- **Hero:** Neighborhood name. Optional image.
- **Intro:** What [Neighborhood] is (boundaries, character). Which communities belong (list with links).
- **Listings:** Aggregated from assigned communities. Grid + map.
- **Market:** Link to report for area if available.
- **Internal links:** City, each community in neighborhood, “All [City] neighborhoods.”

**Content brief**
- [ ] Neighborhood name and city
- [ ] Short description (what it is, who it’s for)
- [ ] List of communities assigned to this neighborhood
- [ ] Keywords: “[Neighborhood] real estate,” “[Neighborhood] [City],” “homes in [Neighborhood]”

## 6. Market Report Page

**Purpose:** Authority and backlinks. Rank for “[Area] market report,” “housing market [City],” “Bend Oregon real estate statistics.”

**URL pattern:** e.g. `/real-estate/oregon/bend/market-report/`, or by tier (city/community) and optionally time.

**Metadata**
- **Title:** `[Area] Housing Market Report | [Time period] | Ryan Realty`
- **Meta description:** [Area] real estate market [time period]: [2–3 key stats]. Median price, sales volume, days on market. Updated [frequency]. (≤155 chars.)

**Structured data:** Report or Dataset, BreadcrumbList, Organization. Optional Chart/Table for key metrics.

**On-page content**
- **Headline:** Area + time period (e.g. “Bend Oregon Housing Market — Last 12 Months”).
- **Summary:** 2–4 sentences in plain language. Key takeaway (e.g. “Prices up X% YoY; inventory tight.”).
- **Charts/tables:** Median/avg price, $/sqft, DOM, list-to-sale, active/new/closed counts, price reductions, absorption, YoY, sold volume. Mobile-friendly, interactive where possible.
- **Interpretation:** Short bullet or paragraph per metric. No jargon.
- **Internal links:** Listings in this area, community/city pages, “View full report,” “Subscribe to reports.”

**Content brief**
- [ ] Geographic scope (state/city/neighborhood/community)
- [ ] Time period
- [ ] Data source and update frequency
- [ ] Key stats to highlight in summary
- [ ] Keywords: “[Area] market report,” “[Area] housing market,” “real estate statistics [Area]”

## 7. Activity Feed (Card Copy)

**Purpose:** Clear, scannable labels. No long copy.

**Per card**
- **Recently Listed:** “Just listed” + [address or community] + price. Optional: “X bed, Y bath.”
- **Just Sold:** “Sold” + [address or community] + sale price (if allowed).
- **Price Drop:** “Price drop” + [address or community] + old → new price.
- **Open House:** “Open house” + [address or community] + date/time + price.

**Content brief**
- [ ] Tone: short, factual, slightly premium.
- [ ] No invented adjectives; let price and stats carry weight.
- [ ] Ensure compliance with Oregon real estate advertising rules.
