# LinkedIn — 4 Pillars of Real Estate Wealth

## Caption (copy + paste — LinkedIn rewards 5-8 line broker-POV posts)

Most owner-occupants who ask me about buying a rental are doing the math wrong.

They look at cash flow alone. ~$200/mo on a $500K property doesn't sound like much, and they pass.

The miss: cash flow is one of four wealth engines that run in parallel on the same property.

On a $500K rental at 25% down, 7% mortgage, 3% long-run appreciation, 24% bracket:

→ Cash flow ~$200/mo (~$2.4K/yr)
→ Appreciation ~$15K in year one (~$403K through year 20)
→ Loan paydown ~$3.8K in year one (~$160K through year 20, funded by your tenant)
→ Depreciation tax savings ~$3.5K/yr (~$70K through year 20)

Stack them: ~$76K of equity at year 3. ~$284K at year 10. ~$681K at year 20.

That's not luck. It's four things compounding at the same time on the same balance sheet.

The decision isn't "is the cash flow worth it." The decision is whether you want all four engines running for you.

(Illustrative numbers, not forecast. Your tax situation will differ.)
