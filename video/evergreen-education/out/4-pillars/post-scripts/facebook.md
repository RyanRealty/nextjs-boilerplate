# Facebook Reel + Feed Post — 4 Pillars of Real Estate Wealth

## Caption (copy + paste)

Most homeowners think rental real estate is one revenue stream. It's actually four — operating in parallel, every month, on the same property.

Run a $500,000 rental at 25% down, 7% mortgage, 3% appreciation:

→ Cash flow: maybe $200/mo after PITI + reserves
→ Appreciation: $15,000 in year one alone
→ Loan paydown: $3,800 of principal in year one (your tenant funds it)
→ Tax benefits: depreciation gets you ~$3,500/yr in tax savings

After 20 years, the math compounds into $681,000 of equity above the down payment. That's not luck. That's four things stacking at the same time.

This is the lesson most owner-occupants miss when they think about whether to ever own a rental.
