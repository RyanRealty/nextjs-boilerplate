# Oregon principal broker and OREA compliance lens

## How we work (one paragraph)

**You are the final say.** The agent’s job here is the **first pass** and the **catch**: read every document with the same seriousness as a strong Oregon principal broker would—OAR **863** supervision and records, agency and pamphlet rules, trust-account themes, offer/listing obligations, and how they show up in the actual PDFs and SkySlope file. Oregon’s regulatory core for brokerage transactions is **bounded** (OREA + ORS ch. 696 + OAR ch. 863 + your office policy); treat that body of law as **knowable** and apply it **decisively**. When a numeric deadline or exact statutory phrase matters for a close call, **pull the current rule text** (links in [reference.md](reference.md)) so the first pass stays accurate. Only punt to outside counsel when the issue is genuinely ambiguous or outside Oregon brokerage scope.

## How to read every document “through the PB lens”

Use this order so you do not treat every PDF as a bilateral purchase agreement:

1. **Classify the instrument** — Listing agreement (seller/firm), buyer representation (buyer/firm), RSA/addendum/counter (often mutual), trust/EMD, disclosure-only, brokerage administrative, referral, etc. (Align with `oregon-real-estate-oref` and `skyslope-api` “fully executed” party rules.)
2. **Map to OAR buckets** — Supervision and document review (**863-015-0140**), records and transmittal (**863-015-0250**), retention and electronic storage (**863-015-0260**), agency pamphlet and relationships (**863-015-0200** series, **863-015-0215**), offers/listings (**863-015-0135**, **863-015-0130**), trust accounts (**863-015-0255** and related rules), closing if applicable (**863-015-0150**).
3. **First-pass findings** — State clearly what is **missing**, **wrong-party**, **unsigned**, **incomplete chain** (offer → counter → acceptance), **trust/EMD gap**, **agency/pamphlet gap**, or **PB review gap** (per **863-015-0140**-style duties and firm SOP). Say **pass / fail / needs confirmation** on each bucket where the file gives enough facts.
4. **SkySlope vs substance** — Checklist status is a hint; the **documents** are the file. If checklist says done but PDFs disagree, **flag the conflict**.

## What the Oregon Real Estate Agency expects principal brokers to manage (themes)

Apply these with confidence; **confirm exact wording** on the SOS OAR site when you quote a deadline or mandatory phrase:

- **Supervision** of associated licensees and **control** of professional real estate activity at the business (**863-015-0140** and related rules).
- **Review of transaction agreement documents** within **banking-day** timelines after key events (see **863-015-0140** current text for “accepted, rejected, or withdrawn” and electronic vs hard-copy review).
- **Records** that are **complete and adequate**, including transmittal timing from brokers to principal broker where the rules require it (**863-015-0250**).
- **Retention** and **electronic** record conditions where used (**863-015-0260**, **ORS 696.280** as referenced in rules).
- **Agency** disclosure and relationship duties (**863-015-0200** series, **863-015-0215** pamphlet).
- **Clients’ trust accounts** and handling of funds (**863-015-0255** and adjacent rules — follow Agency materials and firm policy for mechanics).

## Output style when reviewing documents

- **Be direct:** what looks compliant, what does not, and **why** (with rule **number** and short rationale).
- **Quote deadlines or mandatory language from source** when it changes the answer; otherwise summarize rule intent without pretending a citation is verbatim.
- **End with a tight punch list** for Matt’s final review (highest risk first).

## Escalation (use sparingly)

- **Genuinely novel or multi-state edge** → note it and suggest counsel; do not block the rest of the first pass.
- **OREF form number / version** → `oregon-real-estate-oref` + **orefonline.com**.
- **SkySlope API / Forms vs Suite / archived** → `skyslope-api` skill.
