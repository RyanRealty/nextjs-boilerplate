# Ryan Realty: Creative Intelligence Skill

## When to Use

Use this skill for EVERY creative decision in the content pipeline. Before choosing a visual treatment, text layout, color scheme, motion style, format, or thumbnail approach — consult this skill. This is the agent's creative brain.

This skill does NOT replace:
- `viral-video-quality-gate.md` (hard gate on AI video — still mandatory)
- `viral-hook-library.md` (hook copy — still the hook source)
- `automated-content-pipeline.md` (production pipeline — still the operating system)
- Brand voice enforcement (still runs on every caption)

This skill ADDS the visual and creative layer those skills don't cover: what makes content look good, feel native, stop the scroll, and earn shares.

---

## Core Creative Philosophy

**Ryan Realty content must feel like it was made by someone who lives in Bend and loves data — not by a marketing agency.**

Three creative non-negotiables:
1. **Authenticity over polish.** Raw, real, personal > clean corporate animation. The most-shared real estate content on Instagram looks handmade, not templated.
2. **Data is the hook.** Stats are the magnet. Visuals serve the stat, not the other way around. Every piece of content must teach something or reveal something.
3. **Platform-native always.** Content should look like it belongs in the feed, not like an ad that got dropped in. If a viewer can tell it's "produced," the creative failed.

## Visual Design System

### Color Rules

**Primary palette (every piece):**
- Dark backgrounds preferred (navy #102742, charcoal #1a1a2e, deep slate #0f172a)
- High-contrast accent text (white, gold #c9a84c, electric teal #00d4aa for callouts)
- Maximum 3-4 colors per piece — constraint breeds clarity

**Why dark backgrounds:** 85% of social video is watched on phones, often in dark mode. Dark backgrounds feel native to the feed. Light/white backgrounds scream "ad" and get scrolled past.

**Color psychology for real estate content:**
- Navy/deep blue = trust, authority, professionalism (primary brand color — use it)
- Gold = premium, aspiration, warmth (accent — sparingly)
- Green = growth, money, positive change (for "market up" stats)
- Red/warm amber = urgency, attention, decline (for "market down" or alert stats)
- White = clarity, clean data, breathing room (text on dark, never as background)

**Forbidden:**
- Pastel anything (reads as lifestyle blogger, not market authority)
- Neon overload (one accent neon max per piece)
- Rainbow/gradient chaos (gradients are fine, but only 2-color gradients)
- Pure black backgrounds (too harsh — use near-black with color depth)

### Typography Rules

**Hierarchy (every frame must have exactly one of these):**
1. **Hero stat** — the number or word that stops the scroll. Largest element. 80-120pt equivalent on 1080x1920. Bold weight. Center or slight offset.
2. **Context line** — what the hero stat means. 32-48pt. Directly above or below hero stat. Regular or medium weight.
3. **Source/detail** — supporting info, city name, date range. 18-24pt. Bottom third. Muted color (60% opacity white or gold).

**Font behavior:**
- Sans-serif only for stat cards and data content (AzoSans is the brand body font — use it)
- Display/headline font (Butcher) reserved for hook text and hero statements
- Never more than 2 fonts per frame
- Minimum text size: 24pt on 1080x1920 (anything smaller is unreadable on mobile)

**Text positioning (the 80% safe zone):**
- All critical text lives in the center 80% of the frame (both axes)
- Top 10% and bottom 10% are dead zones (covered by platform UI: username, caption, controls)
- Left/right 10% margins — never bleed text to edge
- For IG Reels specifically: bottom 20% is caption overlay territory — keep it clear or use muted background there

### Composition Rules

**The 3-layer stack (for stat card content):**
1. Background layer: gradient, abstract texture, or cinematic still (never blank)
2. Data layer: the stat, chart, or number
3. Context layer: location tag, date, source

**Visual weight distribution:**
- Eye enters top-left, scans Z-pattern or F-pattern
- Place hero stat at first fixation point (top-third center or slight left)
- Supporting info follows reading gravity (below and right)
- CTA/DM prompt always at final fixation point (bottom center)

**Whitespace:**
- More than you think. Stat cards that breathe outperform cramped ones.
- Minimum 40px padding from any text to any edge or other element
- When in doubt, make the stat bigger and remove something else

## Motion & Animation Principles

### Kinetic Typography (42% higher completion rate)

**Rules for animated text:**
- Animate text sequentially, never all at once (word-by-word or line-by-line reveals)
- Hook word animates first, supporting context follows 0.3-0.5s later
- Use scale-up entrance (80% → 100%) with slight ease-out — feels organic
- End each text animation with 0.5s of stillness before the next element moves
- Total text animation per scene: 1.5-2.5 seconds (longer = viewer disengages)

**Motion types ranked by effectiveness:**
1. Scale-up reveal (word grows from small to full size) — highest retention
2. Slide-in from bottom (text rises into frame) — clean, native feel
3. Fade-in with slight upward drift — subtle, professional
4. Typewriter/letter-by-letter — good for hooks, too slow for data
5. Hard cut appearance (text just appears) — use for rapid-fire stat sequences only

**Forbidden motion:**
- Spin/rotate entrances (reads as 2015 PowerPoint)
- Bounce effects (reads as children's content)
- Excessive particle effects (reads as AI slop)
- 3D text rotation (reads as amateur After Effects)
- Anything that draws attention to the animation itself rather than the content

### Ken Burns Motion (on static frames)

**Standard parameters for listing photos and scenery:**
- Zoom range: 1.0x → 1.08x over 2-3 seconds (subtle push-in)
- Pan: maximum 5% frame shift in any direction
- Always push IN (zoom forward), never pull out (feels retreating)
- Exception: wide establishing shots can pull out to reveal scale

**For stat cards:**
- Minimal motion: 1.0x → 1.03x (barely perceptible)
- The data is the focus, not the movement
- Slight upward drift (1-2% over scene duration) creates subliminal energy

### Scene Transitions

**Preferred (in order):**
1. Hard cut (0ms) — fastest, most energetic, best for beat-synced content
2. Cross-dissolve (200-400ms) — for topic shifts or mood changes
3. Whip/swipe transition — for "before/after" or "then/now" reveals only

**Forbidden:**
- Star wipes, circle reveals, page turns (reads as amateur)
- Zoom transitions (overused, reads as TikTok template)
- Any transition longer than 500ms (kills pacing)

### Scene Duration (The Pacing Engine)

**Optimal scene lengths by content type:**
- Stat card in a rapid sequence: 1.5-2.0s per scene
- Stat card with context (needs reading time): 2.5-3.5s
- Listing photo reveal: 3.0-4.0s
- Neighborhood establishing shot: 2.5-3.5s
- Hook/opening frame: 1.0-1.5s (fast to set the tone)
- CTA/closing frame: 2.0-3.0s (needs dwell time)

**Pacing rules:**
- Never hold a static frame longer than 4 seconds (viewer assumes video froze)
- Vary scene length: alternate short (1.5s) and medium (3s) to create rhythm
- Beat-synced content: scene cuts land on downbeats, text reveals on upbeats
- Acceleration toward the end: scenes get 10-20% shorter in the final third

## Scroll-Stop Techniques

### Pattern Interrupts (1.7-second scroll decision window)

The viewer decides to stop or keep scrolling in 1.7 seconds. Every piece of content must deploy at least one interrupt in frame 1.

**Tier 1 interrupts (strongest — use these):**
- **Contrarian claim**: "Bend's market ISN'T crashing" — challenges assumption, forces engagement
- **Specific number in isolation**: "$847,000" alone on dark background — number without context creates curiosity gap
- **Before/after split frame**: two stats side by side with a divider — comparison is irresistible
- **Question that implies the viewer is wrong**: "Think you can't afford Bend?" — ego engagement

**Tier 2 interrupts (effective — use in rotation):**
- **Unexpected visual texture**: macro nature shot, abstract data viz, aerial Cascade mountain — not what you expect from a realtor
- **Bold typography on dark**: oversized white text on navy/black — breaks the feed's visual pattern
- **Stat with emoji or symbol accent**: 📈 or ↑ next to a number — adds visual energy without clutter

**Tier 3 interrupts (functional — use when Tier 1/2 don't fit):**
- **Color pop**: single bright element (gold stat) against monochrome background
- **Motion in frame 1**: subtle zoom starting immediately — motion in a sea of static thumbnails

### Sound-Off Design (85% watch muted)

Every piece of content must be 100% comprehensible with sound off:
- All spoken words must have text overlay equivalents
- Music is an enhancer, never a carrier of meaning
- Sound effects are bonus, not required for understanding
- Text overlays increase engagement by 50% vs audio-only messaging
- Design the muted experience first, then add audio as a layer

### Thumbnail / First Frame Strategy

The first frame IS the thumbnail on most platforms. Design it as a standalone image:
- Must be compelling as a still photo (it's what appears in the grid and search)
- Hero text must be readable at 1/4 size (how it appears in profile grid)
- No small text, no busy compositions — one bold element per thumbnail
- Faces increase click-through by 38% — but we don't use faces in AI video (per quality gate), so use bold stats and Bend scenery instead

## Platform-Native Aesthetics

### What "Native" Looks Like (April 2026)

**Instagram Reels:**
- Clean, slightly editorial feel. Not overly produced but not sloppy.
- Text overlays with subtle shadows or background pills for readability
- Trending: split-screen comparisons, stat reveal sequences, "POV:" format for education
- Anti-trend: heavy filters, overproduced transitions, corporate look
- The top real estate creators (Mike Hege, 85M views; Lisa Dubois, 12M views) use authenticity + humor + specific data. Not polish.

**TikTok:**
- Raw, fast, opinionated. TikTok rewards personality and speed.
- Text-heavy (entire narrative told through text overlays)
- Trending: green screen over articles/screenshots, "Day in the life" for agents, numbered lists with reveals
- Anti-trend: anything that looks like it was made in a boardroom
- TikTok SEO: include searchable keywords in text overlay AND caption (not just hashtags)

**Facebook Reels:**
- Slightly more polished than TikTok, slightly less editorial than IG
- Wider age demo — make stats contextual ("Here's what this means for your home's value")
- Trending: educational explainers, before/after market comparisons
- Anti-trend: TikTok watermarks (Facebook suppresses these), overly young aesthetic

**YouTube Shorts:**
- Highest information density tolerance. Viewers expect to learn something.
- Trending: rapid-fire fact lists, data breakdowns, myth-busting
- Include strong titles (YouTube Shorts have searchable titles unlike Reels)

## Content Format Intelligence

### Formats Ranked by Performance (Real Estate, Organic)

Based on engagement data from top-performing real estate creators (2025-2026):

1. **Stat reveal sequence** (15-30s) — Market stats revealed one at a time with beat-synced cuts. Highest save rate. Educational + shareable. This is the Friday Hype Reel format.

2. **"Did you know?" education** (15-45s) — Single surprising fact expanded with context. Highest share rate. Works on every platform.

3. **Market comparison** (20-45s) — "Last year vs. this year" or "Bend vs. Portland" split data. Strong save + share. Visual before/after.

4. **Listing dream reveal** (15-30s) — Cinematic photo sequence with price/detail reveal. Aspirational. Drives DMs from buyers.

5. **Neighborhood spotlight** (30-60s) — Scenery + stats + lifestyle positioning. Local audience builder. Longer format acceptable because viewers are exploring.

6. **Quick tip** (10-20s) — One actionable insight, text-heavy, fast. High completion rate due to brevity. Volume play.

7. **Myth buster** (15-30s) — "Everyone thinks X, but actually Y." Contrarian hook + data proof. Strong for comments/debate.

8. **Trending format adaptation** (variable) — Take a viral format and apply it to Bend real estate. Highest ceiling but lowest floor. Only worth doing if the adaptation is genuinely clever, not forced.

### Formats to AVOID

- **Talking head without Synthesia** — We don't have Matt on camera for regular content. Don't produce content that needs a face and doesn't have one.
- **House tours from AI video** — Per quality gate: never use AI-generated video of houses/interiors. Real photos only for real property.
- **"Day in the life" without real footage** — Can't fake authenticity. Skip this until we have real footage.
- **Meme-only content** — One meme per week max. Memes don't build authority. They get views but not leads.
- **Testimonial content** — Only with real client quotes (from reviews/emails). Never fabricate.

## Creative Decision Framework

When the agent receives a content directive (or is generating autonomously from the calendar), follow this decision tree:

### Step 1: What's the content pillar?
Map to: Education, Local Lifestyle, Market Commentary, Personality/Social Proof, or Listings.

### Step 2: What's the primary emotion?
Every piece needs exactly one:
- **Surprise** → stat shock, contrarian fact, unexpected comparison
- **Aspiration** → lifestyle, dream home, "imagine living here"
- **Confidence** → market knowledge, "here's what smart buyers do"
- **Urgency** → market movement, inventory changes, rate shifts
- **Pride** → Bend community, local identity, "this is why we live here"

### Step 3: What format fits?
Use the Format-Platform Fit Matrix above. Choose the format that serves both the pillar and the emotion.

### Step 4: What's the hook?
Pull from `viral-hook-library.md`. The hook must match the emotion:
- Surprise → stat shock hook
- Aspiration → "Would you live here?" or scene-setter hook
- Confidence → "Here's what most people don't know" hook
- Urgency → "This just happened" or "Before you buy/sell" hook
- Pride → Bend-specific cultural hook

### Step 5: What's the visual treatment?
Apply the Visual Design System rules above:
- Choose background (dark gradient, abstract texture, or photo)
- Set color accent based on emotion (green=growth, gold=premium, red=urgency, teal=fresh insight)
- Set typography hierarchy (hero stat + context + source)
- Set motion approach (kinetic text style, scene pacing, transition type)

### Step 6: Run the gates
Before any asset is produced:
- [ ] Viral Video Quality Gate cleared (if AI video involved)
- [ ] Brand voice enforcement passed on all copy
- [ ] Hook from approved library (or new hook reviewed against forbidden patterns)
- [ ] DM CTA included
- [ ] No logo in video frame
- [ ] No agent names in shoutouts
- [ ] Sound-off comprehensible
- [ ] Text in 80% safe zone
- [ ] Maximum 3-4 colors used
- [ ] Dark background (or justified exception)

### Faceless / Data-Driven Account Principles

Since most Ryan Realty content is data-driven without Matt on camera, these principles from successful faceless accounts apply:

1. **Consistent visual identity** — Every post should be instantly recognizable as Ryan Realty without needing a logo. Color palette + typography + composition = brand recognition.
2. **Repeatable format templates** — The audience returns because they know what to expect. The Friday Hype Reel IS a format. Market commentary IS a format. Build 5-7 recognizable formats and rotate.
3. **Earn saves and shares, not just likes** — Saves and shares are 3-5x more valuable than likes for algorithmic distribution. Educational + data content earns these.
4. **Post consistently** — 3-5 Reels per week minimum. The algorithm rewards frequency and consistency more than any single viral hit.
5. **Niche down** — "Bend Oregon real estate data" is a niche. Own it completely rather than being generically about real estate.
