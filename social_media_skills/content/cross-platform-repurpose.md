# Ryan Realty: Cross-Platform Content Repurposing Skill

## When to Use
Use this skill every time a new source asset arrives (listing photos, market data, Snowdrift neighborhood video, or Matt's talking-head recording). One source becomes multiple platform-native pieces without algorithm-penalized duplication.

## Before Starting
Read `Organic_Growth_Intelligence.md` (BRAND MANAGER folder) for cross-platform strategy and algorithm suppression rules.

---

## The Repurposing Pyramid

One source asset flows through a five-tier system:

**Tier 1 (Primary Format)**: Original format optimized for the strongest algorithm signal.
**Tier 2 (Native Spin-Offs)**: Same content, re-edited for platform-specific native formats.
**Tier 3 (Carousel & Stories)**: Multi-part content for IG feed and Stories.
**Tier 4 (Text & Social)**: LinkedIn, email, newsletter versions.
**Tier 5 (Archive & Reference)**: Slug for future repurposing and seasonal refresh.

## Source-to-Output Maps

### Source: One Listing (Property Photos + Video from Rich / Framed Visuals)

**Tier 1 Primary**: IG Reel (9:16 vertical, 15-90 sec, trending audio, brand-clean)
- Hook: Listing name + price OR neighborhood feature
- Retention beat structure: First 3 sec hook, 7 sec value/visuals, 15 sec close-up tour, final frame CTA
- DM CTA: "DM me 'tour'"
- Audio: Trending audio from this week's audio log

**Tier 2 Native Spin-Offs**:
1. **TikTok (9:16, same length)**: Strip Instagram watermark. Re-grade color slightly warmer (TikTok audience skews younger, prefers warmer tone). Keep script identical, keep trending audio. Post with location tag (Bend, Oregon) + property address.

2. **Facebook Reel (1:1 or 9:16)**: Crop to 1:1 (square). Enable "Cross-Post to Instagram" if on same account. Alternate: Re-edit to 9:16 and post natively to FB. Use same script and audio. FB audience is older, more information-seeking, add listing address and basic details (beds, baths, price) as on-screen text overlays.

3. **YouTube Short (9:16, under 60 sec)**: Upload Reel directly. Add YouTube-specific title as search query (e.g., "Luxury Home for Sale Bend Oregon $1.6M Acreage"). Trending audio works here too. Enable pinned comment with property link.

**Tier 3 Multi-Part Content**:
1. **IG Carousel (8 frames, 4:5 or 1:1)**: Frame 1: Hook (price + neighborhood). Frames 2-5: Key rooms / views (bedroom, kitchen, living, outdoor). Frames 6-7: Market context (price per sq ft, neighborhood data). Frame 8: CTA + listing link. Static images only, no video. Different vibe from Reel.

2. **IG Story (7-frame sequence)**:
   - Frame 1: Hook ("Would you live here?")
   - Frames 2-4: Property showcase (one room per frame, tight crops)
   - Frames 5-6: Price reveal + key stat
   - Frame 7: CTA sticker (link or DM)
   Each frame 2-3 seconds. Stickers encouraged (location tag, poll, DM sticker).

**Tier 4 Text & Social**:
1. **LinkedIn Native Video**: Upload the Reel directly to LinkedIn. LinkedIn's text-first audience wants education + lifestyle. Caption should open with a question or insight, not a hard sell. Example: "In Bend's luxury market right now, location matters more than square footage. Here's why." Then embed the video.

2. **Google Business "What's New"**: Upload one still image from the listing (most striking view). Add 150-character caption. Google Business visibility feeds local search and Maps results. Example caption: "NEW LISTING: Stunning Tumalo acreage with mountain views. $1.6M. Schedule your tour."

3. **Email Listing Announcement**: Send to Follow Up Boss lead list. Use one Reel thumbnail as hero image, embed the Reel video link (or YouTube link). Shorter email body: 2-3 sentences hook, video embed, button link to full listing.

**Tier 5 Archive**: Save listing slug (`tumalo_19496_reel_april2026.mp4` + captions file) for seasonal refresh. November: resend email "Still Available: Tumalo Acreage" with same Reel. Summer: create "6 Months on Market" follow-up video using same footage.

### Source: One Market Statistic (Supabase Data or Weekly Market Report)

**Tier 1 Primary**: IG Reel (9:16, 30-45 sec, talking-head + B-roll)
- Hook: Lead with the number (bold on screen + spoken aloud)
- Format: Matt on camera (or TBA when Synthesia avatar ready) + data visualization B-roll
- Audio: Background music from trending audio log (lo-fi, cinematic warm)
- Script: Stat reveal (3 sec), explanation (15 sec), implication for buyer/seller (20 sec), CTA (5 sec)
- DM CTA: "DM me 'market'"

**Tier 2 Native Spin-Offs**:
1. **TikTok (9:16)**: Same video, same script. Add on-screen text that says "Bend Real Estate Update" (TikTok audience expects rapid-cut edits, so increase cut rate by 20% vs IG version). Keep audio.

2. **Instagram Static Post (4:5 or 1:1)**: Carve one frame from the video (the data visualization moment). Add 3-5 sentence caption that restates the hook + implication. Include same DM CTA. Example caption: "Bend's inventory just shifted. We're seeing 34 percent more homes on market than last month. Here's what that means if you're buying or selling right now. DM me 'market'."

3. **YouTube Short (same as Reel)**: Post the talking-head video. Title: "[STAT] Bend Real Estate Update [Month]". Pinned comment: Link to full market report.

**Tier 3 Multi-Part Content**:
1. **IG Story (4-frame)**: Frame 1: Big number with shock visual ("Inventory UP 34%"). Frame 2: Comparison chart (before/after). Frame 3: "What this means..." (implication). Frame 4: DM sticker for market guide.

2. **IG Carousel (5 frames)**: Frame 1: Hook stat. Frames 2-4: Breakdown data (neighborhoods, price points, days-on-market). Frame 5: CTA to DM for full analysis.

**Tier 4 Text & Social**:
1. **LinkedIn Text Post**: Open with question. "Is Bend cooling or just recalibrating?" Then state the stat and link to a longer analysis. Post natively as text, no embedded video (LinkedIn's algorithm favors native text over external links). Example:

   "This month's Bend market snapshot tells an interesting story.
   
   Inventory is up 34 percent. Days-on-market stretched to 51. But price stays firm.
   
   What does this mean? [Link to full analysis]"

2. **Weekly Email (Follow Up Boss cadence)**: Lead with the stat as subject line or preview text. Body: 2-3 sentence explanation. Embed the Reel link. CTA: "Read the full market report."

3. **Newsletter Bullet**: If Matt sends a weekly newsletter, stat becomes one bullet under "This Week in Bend Real Estate."

**Tier 5 Archive**: Save the stat + script for future comparisons. Example: Six months later, "Inventory was up 34 percent in April. Here's where we are now."

### Source: One Snowdrift Neighborhood Video (19 available)

**Tier 1 Primary**: IG Reel (9:16, 30-60 sec, Snowdrift B-roll)
- Hook: "[Neighborhood name] in 60 seconds" OR "This neighborhood has [differentiator]"
- Visuals: Professional Snowdrift Visuals footage (streets, trails, parks, downtown, homes)
- Audio: Trending audio (warm, cinematic, folk-acoustic preferred)
- Script: Hook (3 sec), 3-4 differentiators with visuals (30-50 sec), CTA (5 sec)
- DM CTA: "DM me '[Neighborhood Name]'" or "DM me 'Bend'"

**Tier 2 Native Spin-Offs**:
1. **TikTok (9:16)**: Same Snowdrift footage. Keep audio. Add on-screen text: "Neighborhood Spotlight: [Name]". TikTok audience expects text overlays to feel more casual, use quirky font choices or animated text.

2. **YouTube Short**: Snowdrift video + voiceover narration. Title: "Walking Through [Neighborhood] Bend Oregon." Pinned comment: "Best trails near [neighborhood]" + "Schools in [neighborhood]" links.

3. **Facebook Post (Video or Carousel)**: Post the Reel as FB native video. Longer caption with full neighborhood details (schools, recreation, restaurants, market data).

**Tier 3 Multi-Part Content**:
1. **IG Story (5-frame series)**:
   - Frame 1: "Know [Neighborhood]?" (intro question)
   - Frames 2-3: Trails / outdoor recreation (Snowdrift B-roll)
   - Frame 4: "Food + Drink" (downtown/restaurants clip from Snowdrift)
   - Frame 5: "DM me for homes in this area" + link sticker

2. **IG Carousel (7 frames)**:
   - Frame 1: Neighborhood name + hook stat (e.g., "Most walkable neighborhood in Bend")
   - Frames 2-4: Scenic stills from Snowdrift video (trails, parks, downtown)
   - Frames 5-6: Lifestyle/food photo + school/family info
   - Frame 7: CTA to DM for available listings

**Tier 4 Text & Social**:
1. **LinkedIn Post**: Frame as "Why [Neighborhood] is Bend's Hidden Gem." Use Snowdrift still as header image. Write 3-4 sentence thought piece about what makes the area unique. Keep it authentic, not a sales pitch.

2. **Google Business Post (What's New)**: Upload one striking Snowdrift still. Caption: "[Neighborhood]: [One-line description of vibe]. Explore available homes." Link to neighborhood guide if you build one.

3. **Email or Newsletter**: "Spotlight: [Neighborhood]" as section header. Embed Snowdrift video. Add 3-4 bullet points (schools, trails, vibe). CTA: "DM me to schedule a tour."

**Tier 5 Archive & Seasonal Refresh**: Save the neighborhood slug. Reuse every 3-4 months as "neighborhood spotlight rotation." Every quarter, pull a different Snowdrift neighborhood video and repurpose it. (You have 19 neighborhoods, so one neighborhood featured every 2 weeks = year-round rotation.)

## Native Re-Edit Rules (Avoid Algorithm Suppression)

### Rule 1: Never Post TikTok-Watermarked Video to IG
If the source is a TikTok video, you MUST remove the TikTok watermark before posting to IG or FB. Algorithm suppresses cross-platform watermarks.

**How to fix**:
1. Download the video from TikTok.
2. Re-render it in CapCut (add a 1-second intro frame or re-color slightly) so it's "new" in IG's system.
3. Upload to IG as fresh content.

### Rule 2: Re-Edit Captions Per Platform
**Instagram**: Hook in the first line. Full context in body. DM CTA at end.
**TikTok**: Hook should be visible on-screen AND spoken in first 3 seconds (TikTok users are skimmers who react to visual + audio cues).
**YouTube Shorts**: Title is a search query (people search YouTube like Google). Hook is on-screen as text.
**LinkedIn**: Open with a question or insight. Professional tone. Video is secondary to the text.
**Facebook**: Full address, full context, option to link to external listing.

Example: Same market stat video.
- **IG caption**: "Inventory just shifted in Bend. We're seeing 34 percent more homes on market. Here's what that means if you're buying or selling right now. DM me 'market'."
- **TikTok caption**: (Spoken in video + on-screen text:) "Bend's inventory UP 34 percent. What this means for you..."
- **LinkedIn caption**: "Is Bend cooling or just recalibrating? New data this week tells an interesting story. [Video link]"
- **Facebook caption**: "NEW MARKET SNAPSHOT: Bend inventory up 34 percent | 51 days on market | Prices holding firm. Full report: [link]"

### Rule 3: Aspect Ratio Lock Per Platform
No letterboxing. No awkward black bars.

**Instagram Reel**: 9:16 (vertical, full height)
**TikTok**: 9:16 (vertical, full height)
**YouTube Shorts**: 9:16 (vertical, full height)
**Facebook**: 9:16 (vertical, full height) OR 1:1 (square, for feed carousel)
**LinkedIn**: 16:9 (horizontal) OR 9:16 (vertical, but less common)

If your source is 16:9 (horizontal), create two versions:
- Horizontal (9:16 with top/bottom title bars) for LinkedIn/YouTube
- Vertical (crop tightly, re-frame for 9:16) for IG/TikTok

### Rule 4: Audio Strategy Per Platform
**Instagram**: Trending audio is algorithm gold (algorithm prioritizes trending audio). Use it.
**TikTok**: Trending audio is algorithm gold. Use it. If using original voiceover (Matt talking), layer low-volume trending music underneath.
**YouTube Shorts**: Trending audio works, but voiceover is equally fine. YouTube Shorts algorithm is less audio-dependent than IG Reels.
**LinkedIn**: Original voiceover or no audio. LinkedIn audience expects professional tone; trending audio can feel out of place.
**Facebook**: Original voiceover or subtle background music. Trending audio is okay but not essential.

## Verification Checklist Before Cross-Posting

Before you publish version 2 of any asset, confirm:

- [ ] Watermarks removed (no IG/TikTok watermark on cross-posts)
- [ ] Captions rewritten for platform (not copy-paste)
- [ ] Aspect ratio correct (no letterboxing)
- [ ] Audio appropriate for platform (trending audio on IG/TikTok; voiceover on LinkedIn)
- [ ] On-screen text re-edited for platform norms (dense text TikTok; minimal text IG)
- [ ] DM CTA or link matches platform best practice
- [ ] Color grade consistent with platform tone (TikTok slightly warmer than IG)
- [ ] Video file size under platform limits (IG Reel max 4GB, TikTok max 287MB)
- [ ] No brand violations (logo, phone, URL only where appropriate)
