# Ryan Realty: Lead Nurture Skill

## When to Use
Use this skill when Matt asks to write email sequences, drip campaigns, follow-up messages, lead magnet content, or any communication designed to build trust with a lead over time — buyers, sellers, or out-of-state homeowners.

## Before Starting
Read `Ryan_Realty_Marketing_Intelligence.md` — specifically the "Lead Generation & Nurture Psychology" section. Understanding the buyer's 3-stage journey and the 5 trust-building blocks is essential before writing any nurture content.

---

## The Core Principle
Lead nurture is NOT about selling. It's about showing up, consistently, as a trusted expert — until they're ready to act. Then you're the obvious choice because you're already the person they trust.

The enemy is: generic, salesy, boring. Every email should feel like it was written for one person, not broadcast to a list.

---

## Lead Segments (Write Differently for Each)

### Segment 1: Early-Stage Buyers (Stage 1: Dreaming)
**Profile**: Browsing casually, not pre-approved, no urgency  
**Goal**: Stay top-of-mind, build credibility, capture attention  
**Cadence**: Monthly (1 email/month, low pressure)  
**Content**: Education + lifestyle + market commentary (never listings yet)

### Segment 2: Active Buyers (Stage 2: Exploring)
**Profile**: Pre-approved or in process, actively looking at neighborhoods/prices  
**Goal**: Position Matt as the local expert, invite conversation  
**Cadence**: Weekly (1-2 emails/week)  
**Content**: Neighborhood guides, market data by price point, comparable sales, what to expect in offers

### Segment 3: Hot Buyers (Stage 3: Deciding)
**Profile**: Actively touring, comparing properties, ready to offer  
**Goal**: Make the conversion — showing, call, offer  
**Cadence**: Daily/real-time (calls, texts, personal emails)  
**Content**: Specific properties, offer strategy, negotiation intel, timeline clarity

### Segment 4: Out-of-State Homeowners (Priority Segment)
**Profile**: Own property in Bend/Central Oregon, live elsewhere (CA, WA, etc.)  
**Goal**: Position Matt as the remote-sale specialist, build trust, surface intent to sell  
**Cadence**: Weekly over 8 weeks, then monthly  
**Content**: See out-of-state drip structure below

### Segment 5: Past Clients (Relationship Maintenance)
**Profile**: People who've already bought or sold with Matt  
**Goal**: Stay top-of-mind for referrals and future transactions  
**Cadence**: Monthly market update + 2-3 personal touchpoints per year  
**Content**: Market updates, local news, genuine check-ins (no pitch)

## Email Sequence Templates

### New Buyer Lead (4-email, 3-week sequence)

**Email 1 (Day 1 — Welcome + Value)**
Subject: "[Name], here's what I'd tell a friend about buying in Bend right now"
- Personal intro (not corporate)
- One honest insight about current market conditions
- Attached or linked: "Bend Buyer's Guide" or "Bend Neighborhood Overview"
- Soft CTA: "Reply with any questions — I answer everything personally"

**Email 2 (Day 5 — Expertise)**
Subject: "What I'm seeing right now in [Neighborhood they're interested in]"
- Specific data about their neighborhood of interest
- Recent sales, days on market, price trends
- Your take on the market in that area
- CTA: "Would it help to schedule a 15-min call to go over what you've seen?"

**Email 3 (Day 12 — Social Proof)**
Subject: "[Client name] just closed on [address] — here's their story"
- 1-2 paragraph client story (with permission / anonymized if needed)
- Focus on the challenge they had and how Matt helped
- Relatable situation (price concern, timing, competitive offer)
- CTA: "I'd love to help you with the same process — [calendar link]"

**Email 4 (Day 21 — Low-Friction CTA)**
Subject: "A question before I follow up next month..."
- Acknowledge they may not be ready yet
- Offer something with zero pressure: "Would a free market analysis on any properties you've been watching be useful?"
- Make it easy to say "not yet" (this builds respect and trust)
- Clearly state: next touch in 30 days unless they want sooner

---

### Out-of-State Homeowner Sequence (8-email, 8-week)

**Week 1 — The Hook**: "You own property in Bend but live in [state]. Here are 3 options..."
- Acknowledge their situation directly (not a form letter)
- Present 3 paths honestly: Hold, rent, sell — no bias
- Ask them to reply which situation fits
- No CTA to sell, just to talk

**Week 2 — Authority**: "Here's what we do differently for out-of-state sellers"
- Explain the remote process (video tours, digital signatures, no flight required)
- 1 specific statistic about out-of-state sales you've handled
- Let the process sell itself

**Week 3 — Social Proof**: "How we sold 3 Bend properties remotely in 90 days"
- Short case study (real or composite, honest)
- Focus on *ease* not profit
- Quote from out-of-state seller if possible

**Week 4 — Education**: "The true cost of holding a Bend property from [state]"
- Taxes, management fees, vacancy, maintenance
- The math: cost of holding vs. market opportunity now
- No pressure — just help them see the numbers

**Week 5 — Market Context**: "This is what the Bend market looks like right now"
- Current median price, days on market, buyer demand
- Favorable seller data (if true)
- Honest take on trajectory

**Week 6 — Objection Handle**: "What if you're not ready to sell yet?"
- Options for holding and maximizing the property
- How to position it for future sale
- Timeline flexibility — no pressure

**Week 7 — Social Proof 2**: "3 more out-of-state sellers, 3 different situations"
- Variety of scenarios (seller who needed speed, seller who needed maximum price, seller who wasn't sure)
- Focus on relief and outcome

**Week 8 — CTA**: "Here's what a conversation looks like (zero commitment)"
- Describe the 15-min call: you talk, they ask questions, no obligation
- Low-friction booking link
- If no response: move to monthly market update cadence

## Email Writing Principles

**Subject Lines That Work**:
- Specific + personal: "What sold in [Neighborhood] last month" (not "Market Update")
- Matt's name + value: "Matt here — I found something you'll want to see"
- Curiosity gap: "Why [Neighborhood] is outperforming the market right now"
- Avoid: "Weekly Update," "Check Out These Homes," anything with exclamation points

**Body Copy Rules**:
- First sentence is everything — if it's boring, they stop reading
- Write like you're writing to one specific person (even if it's going to 500)
- 150-300 words is ideal (readable on mobile in 90 seconds)
- One primary CTA per email (not three)
- End with something personal — a question, an observation, a genuine offer to help
- Never start with "I" (leads with you, not the reader)

**Matt's Voice**:
- Direct. Warm. Knowledgeable. Unhurried.
- Never: corporate speak, forced urgency, hollow phrases
- Always: specific, honest, writes like a person not a system
