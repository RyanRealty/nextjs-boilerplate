# Ryan Realty: Ads Management Skill

## When to Use
Use this skill when Matt asks to set up, manage, review, optimize, or advise on any paid advertising — Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram), Google Ads (Search/Display/Performance Max), Google Local Services Ads (LSA), or website monetization (AdSense).

1. Read `Paid_Ads_Intelligence.md` (BRAND MANAGER folder) — the complete reference for all platform mechanics, benchmarks, targeting, and strategy
2. **Always do a web search to verify current platform interface before giving setup instructions** — Meta Ads Manager and Google Ads interfaces change frequently. Never assume you know the current UI steps from training data alone.

## Meta Ads Workflow

### Step 1: Establish the Goal First
Before touching Meta Ads Manager, identify:
- What is the ONE goal of this campaign? (Lead generation / Listing exposure / Brand awareness / Retargeting)
- Who is the target audience? (Local Bend buyers / Out-of-state relocators / Past website visitors / Email list)
- What is the budget? (Minimum viable: $300-500/month to generate any data; $800-1500/month to produce consistent leads)
- What is the conversion action? (Form fill / DM / Phone call / Website visit)

**Match objective to goal:**
| Goal | Campaign Objective |
|------|-------------------|
| Collect leads directly in Facebook | Lead Generation |
| Drive website traffic to listing page | Traffic or Conversions |
| Retarget website visitors | Conversions (with Pixel) |
| Get DMs/messages from prospects | Messages |
| Build brand awareness, video views | Video Views or Brand Awareness |

### Step 2: Audience Setup
Real estate Fair Housing rules RESTRICT targeting by: race, color, religion, sex, national origin, familial status, disability.

**What you CAN target:**
- Geographic: City, zip codes, radius (use Bend, OR + surrounding areas)
- Interest-based: Home buyers, homeowners, real estate, mortgage rates, HGTV, Zillow users
- Behavioral: Likely to move, recently moved
- Custom Audiences: Email list upload, website visitors (requires Pixel), video viewers, Instagram/Facebook engagers
- Lookalike Audiences: Built from your email list or best lead segment (1-3% lookalike for best quality)

**Advantage+ Audiences**: Meta's AI-driven targeting. Use it when you have enough conversion data (50+ conversions). For new campaigns, manually set targeting first to gather data.

### Step 3: Creative Guidelines for Real Estate
- **Video beats static by 2.2x** — Always prefer video (Reel repurpose, property walkthrough, talking head)
- **Lead Form ads** outperform link-click ads for lead generation — form stays within Facebook, lower friction
- **Testimonial creative** outperforms listing-only creative for cold audiences
- **Listing showcase** (carousel with multiple property photos) works for warm/retargeting audiences
- Recommended creative tests: (1) Talking head vs. (2) Property video vs. (3) Testimonial
- Headlines: Specific beats generic. "Homes under $600k in Bend — See Today's Listings" beats "Find Your Dream Home"

### Step 4: Budget & Bidding
- Start with: $15-25/day per ad set (enough to generate data within 1 week)
- Bidding: Start with "Maximize Leads" or "Lowest Cost" — let Meta optimize before switching to Target Cost
- Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO): Let Meta distribute budget across ad sets once you have 2+ running
- Learning Phase: Meta needs 50 optimization events per week to exit learning — expect 2 weeks before optimizing

### Step 5: Measurement & Optimization
Key metrics to track:
| Metric | Good | Needs Work |
|--------|------|-----------|
| CPL (Cost Per Lead) | Under $30 | Over $60 |
| CTR (Click-Through Rate) | Over 1.5% | Under 0.5% |
| Landing Page CVR | Over 3% | Under 1% |
| Video View Rate (3s) | Over 40% | Under 20% |
| Ad Frequency | Under 3 | Over 5 |

Optimization triggers: If CTR is low → test new creative/hook. If CPL is high → test new audience. If high frequency → refresh creative or expand audience.

## Google Ads Workflow

### Step 1: Campaign Type Selection
| Goal | Campaign Type |
|------|--------------|
| Appear when people search "real estate agent Bend" | Search |
| Retarget website visitors with display ads | Display |
| Automated across Search + Display + YouTube | Performance Max |
| Pay only when someone calls or fills lead form | Local Services Ads (LSA) |

**Start with Search** — highest intent, most controllable. Add Performance Max after Search is optimized.

### Step 2: Keyword Strategy
**High-intent buyer keywords** (lower volume, higher conversion):
- "homes for sale Bend Oregon"
- "real estate agent Bend OR"
- "buy house Bend Oregon"
- "Bend Oregon realtor"

**High-intent seller keywords** (most expensive, highest value):
- "sell my home Bend Oregon"
- "home value Bend OR"
- "list my house Bend Oregon"
- "real estate listing agent Bend"

**Negative keywords to add immediately** (prevents wasted spend):
- free, rental, rent, apartment, cheap, commercial, foreclosure (unless you serve those)
- Add broad negative list before launching

**Match types**: Start with Phrase and Exact match only. Avoid Broad match until you have enough conversion data.

### Step 3: Ad Copy Formula for Real Estate
- **Headline 1**: Local + service ("Bend Oregon Real Estate Agent")
- **Headline 2**: Differentiator ("Local Expert, Boutique Service")
- **Headline 3**: CTA + hook ("Free Home Value Report | Call Now")
- **Description**: Lead with benefit + social proof ("Matt Ryan helps Bend buyers and sellers navigate a competitive market. Local expertise, personal service, real results.")

### Step 4: Conversion Tracking (MUST DO BEFORE LAUNCHING)
- Install Google Tag on website
- Track: Phone calls from ads (call extension), form submissions, contact page visits
- Without conversion tracking, you're flying blind — don't spend money until this is set up

### Step 5: Budget & Bidding
- Real estate CPC range in Bend: $2-15 (buyer keywords); $10-65 (seller keywords — "sell my home" is expensive nationally but less so in local small markets)
- Start with: $20-30/day — enough to get 5-10 clicks/day and gather data
- Bidding: Start with "Maximize Clicks" — after 30+ conversions, switch to "Target CPA"

## Google Local Services Ads (LSA) — Real Estate

LSA is different from regular Google Ads: you pay per lead (someone calls or messages you), not per click. Google Guaranteed badge builds trust.

**Setup requirements:**
1. Create LSA account at ads.google.com/localservices
2. Select "Real Estate Agent" as service type
3. Submit for background check and license verification (Google requires this for real estate)
4. Build out profile: headshot, bio, service area, business hours
5. Get Google Guaranteed badge (requires passing verification)

**Cost**: $24-36 CPL in Oregon market (significantly better than Meta or Search Ads for seller leads)

**Optimization**: Reviews are the #1 ranking factor for LSA. Ask every closed client to leave a Google review immediately after closing. 30+ reviews is the target for competitive visibility.

**Lead quality**: Dispute invalid leads through the LSA dashboard (wrong service type, outside service area, etc.) — Google credits disputes, so always audit your leads.

## Google AdSense (Website Monetization)
**Recommendation**: Do NOT use AdSense on the Ryan Realty website. 

The math: AdSense earns roughly $2-5 per 1,000 page views. A captured real estate lead is worth $5,000-10,000+ in potential commission. Showing ads on the site distracts from lead capture, drives visitors away, and damages the professional brand image. The value of a lead conversion far exceeds any ad revenue.

If monetization is desired, focus on: lead capture optimization, not display advertising.
