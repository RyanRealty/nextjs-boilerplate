# Ryan Realty: Community Management Playbook

## When to Use
Invoke this skill when managing Instagram DMs, comment responses, reviews, or any direct engagement with followers. This is the operational playbook for responding to audience in real-time.

## Before Starting
- Brand voice is the foundation. Read BRAND MANAGER/Ryan_Realty_Brand_Voice_And_Tone_Guide.docx
- DM response time is an algorithm signal (Instagram tracks response speed within 1 hour)
- This is relationship-building work, not transaction-closing. Never push sales; serve first.

---

## DM Response Targets & Timing

**Response time SLA (Service Level Agreement):**
- Business hours (9am-5pm Bend time, Mon-Fri): Respond within 1 hour of message received (algorithm signal; faster response = reach boost)
- After hours / weekends: Respond by next morning 9am (not mandatory algorithm signal, but good for relationship)
- Real estate emergencies (showings, offers): Respond within 15 minutes

**Why timing matters:** Instagram algorithm tracks DM response time. Accounts with <1 hour average response time get 20-30% reach boost on posts. This is why DM-CTA is mandatory on every post, early DM volume + fast response = algorithmic flywheel.

## DM Conversation Flow & Capture

**Flow (3-message minimum before capture):**

1. **First message (warm, value-first):** Acknowledge question + offer lead magnet (if keyword match) or direct answer (if specific enough to answer in 1-2 sentences). Always be helpful before asking for contact.

2. **Second message (if needed):** Deliver lead magnet OR ask clarifying question ("Are you looking to buy, sell, or just gathering info?"). Keep tone conversational, not salesy.

3. **Capture (after 2+ messages):** "Would love to send you more relevant resources. Can I grab your email + phone?" OR "Let's schedule a quick call to go deeper, no pressure. [Calendly link]"

**Capture language (non-pushy):**
- "Would you be open to a quick call? Easier to answer your questions live: [Calendly]"
- "Lot of context here, easier on a call. Are you free Thursday at 2pm? [Calendly]"
- "Sending over that guide, want me to walk you through it? Quick 15-min call: [Calendly]"
- Never: "Schedule a call," "Let's set up an appointment," "When's good for you to meet?" (salesy, puts burden on prospect)

**After capture:**
1. Add to Follow Up Boss with tag (tag = which nurture sequence)
2. Drop into mapped nurture sequence (email-based, from BRAND MANAGER/.claude/skills/ryan-realty/lead-nurture.md)
3. Continue DM conversation (don't abandon them post-capture; stay warm 1-2 more messages)

## Comment Response Playbook

**Comment triage rules:**

**Rule 1: Answer-able questions on public comments**
- Respond directly in comment thread
- Keep to 1-3 sentences (public comments shouldn't be essays)
- Answer the question fully (don't create curiosity gap to DM)
- Example question: "How long does closing take?" Example response: "Usually 30-45 days from accepted offer to close. Lots of variables (inspection, appraisal, financing), but that's the standard window."

**Rule 2: Objections / concerns → Deflect to DM**
- If comment is expressing doubt / concern / pushback, respond with: "Great question, DM me and let's dig into that. Easier in a conversation."
- Example: Comment: "Real estate agents are all the same." Response: "Ha, I get that pushback, that's exactly why I left [Franchise] for independent brokerage. DM me 'independent' if you want the full story."
- Deflection goal: Move conversation to DM to capture contact (objection handling = high conversion)

**Rule 3: Trolls / bad-faith comments → Ignore**
- Ignore comments that are purely inflammatory, spam, or designed to antagonize
- Never engage trolls (feeds algorithm negativity)
- Never delete comments (deletion looks defensive; algorithm suppresses posts with heavy commenting-then-deleting)
- Let them sit; algorithm deprioritizes troll comments naturally

**Rule 4: Property-specific comments**
- Comments like "What's the price on [property]?" or "Interested in touring [address]" → Direct link to property page or Aryeo link + "DM me to schedule: [Calendly]"
- Short, helpful, link-rich response

**Comment response time:**
- Respond within 30-60 minutes of comment posted (algorithm signal; fast responses boost comment ranking)
- Early comments get more visibility; responding quickly amplifies reach

## Negative Comments & Review Management

**When a comment is negative but not troll:**

Example: "This agent charged me way too much commission" or "Took forever to sell my house"

Response framework (drafted by agent, approved by Matt before posting):
1. Acknowledge (not apologize reflexively): "Sorry you felt that way about our service."
2. Empathy: "That's feedback we take seriously."
3. Offer offline: "DM me so we can talk through what happened."

Examples:
- Comment: "This agent overpriced my listing and it never sold." Response: "Sorry to hear, that's exactly the kind of feedback we need to hear. DM me and let's talk about what happened."
- Comment: "Worst experience ever." Response: "I'm sorry you had a bad experience. I'd like to understand what went wrong. DM me?"

**Never:**
- Apologize for Matt's business decisions (pricing strategy, marketing approach, etc.)
- Get defensive about fees or commissions in comments (discuss offline in DM)
- Make promises in comments ("I'll fix this") without understanding situation first

**For reviews (Google Business Profile, Yelp, Zillow):**

Same framework: Acknowledge, empathize, offer offline conversation. Example:
- Review: "Slow communication and overpriced." Response: "Thanks for taking the time to review. Communication is something we pride ourselves on, I'd like to understand where we fell short. Please DM me or call 541.213.6706."

## Negative Review Response Protocol

**1-star or 2-star reviews require Matt's personal response** (don't let agent respond on Matt's behalf; owner response carries more weight)

Response framework:
1. **Acknowledge the issue** (not "sorry," but recognition): "I see you had concerns about [specific issue]."
2. **Take accountability if Matt is at fault** (no excuses): "That's on me. Here's what I should have done..."
3. **Explain without defending** (if legitimate business decision): "The pricing strategy was based on [market data], but I understand that didn't meet your expectations."
4. **Offer recovery** (if appropriate): "I'd like to make this right. Can you call me at 541.213.6706?"
5. **Keep offline** (suggest DM or call; don't have entire recovery conversation in review thread)

Example responses:
- **Review:** "Matt didn't communicate during the process." **Response:** "That's not acceptable, communication is the foundation of trust. I should have sent weekly updates. Can you call me at 541.213.6706 so I can understand what happened and make it right?"
- **Review:** "Commission was too high." **Response:** "I hear you. Our commission reflects the level of service we provide (market analysis, targeted marketing, negotiation). But I value your feedback. DM me, I'd like to discuss your concerns."

**Negative review best practices:**
- Respond within 48 hours (Google algorithm favors prompt responses)
- Never get defensive or argue with reviewer
- Longer responses (3-4 sentences) perform better than short dismissals
- Responding to negative reviews increases business profile authority (shows active management)

## Escalation Protocol

**Flag for Matt's immediate review (do NOT respond as agent):**

1. **Legal/compliance questions:**
   - Fair Housing questions ("Can I discriminate based on X?")
   - Disclosure requirements ("Do I have to tell buyers about X?")
   - Agent liability ("Is the agent responsible for X?")
   - Response: "That's a great legal question, I'm flagging this for Matt to answer directly. He'll DM you within 24 hours."

2. **Complaints about Matt personally:**
   - Personal conduct allegations
   - Professional ethics questions
   - Demands for refunds or service recovery
   - Response: "I appreciate you sharing this feedback. I'm looping Matt in so he can address this directly. He'll reach out to you within 24 hours."

3. **Threats or hostile language:**
   - Threats of legal action
   - Aggressive harassment
   - Defamatory statements
   - Response: Do not respond in public. Flag for Matt + potentially legal review. Do not engage further.

**Escalation process:**
- Screenshot the comment/DM
- Create Follow Up Boss task with "ESCALATION, Matt Review Required"
- Tag with issue type (Legal, Complaint, Threat, etc.)
- Matt reviews and determines response strategy

## Response Voice & Personality

**Rules (mandatory):**
- Never canned. Never copy-paste across multiple responders.
- Never "DM me for details" when you have the answer.
- Never corporate. Keep tone warm, conversational, like a trusted friend.
- Never defensive or apologetic for business decisions.
- Never make promises you can't keep in public (move offline).

**Examples of good vs bad:**

❌ **Bad:** "Thank you for your interest in our services. We look forward to connecting with you. Feel free to reach out with any questions."
✓ **Good:** "Thanks for the question! [Answer]. If you want to dig deeper, happy to chat over the phone, [Calendly link]"

❌ **Bad:** "DM me for more information about this listing."
✓ **Good:** "Great question! The property is 2.28 acres with 1500 sqft of living space. Want to schedule a showing? [Calendly link]"

❌ **Bad:** "We apologize for your experience. We take all feedback seriously." [Then silence]
✓ **Good:** "I hear you, that's not the experience we want. DM me so we can talk through what happened."

## DM Volume & Response Metrics

**Track weekly (signals algorithm health):**
- DMs received per post (target: 3-5 per 1k followers for engagement-heavy posts)
- DM response time average (target: <30 min during business hours)
- DM-to-lead conversion (contacts captured per 20 DMs)
- Nurture sequence enrollment (contacts added to Follow Up Boss per week)

**Weekly check-in:**
- If DM volume is dropping, problem is hook or CTA (content is weak)
- If response time is >1 hour average, hiring support or reshuffling hours needed
- If capture rate is <15%, DM flow needs tightening (not enough value delivery before ask)

## Verification Checklist

Before marking DM/comment response complete:

1. **Brand voice check:** Response reads like Matt (warm, knowledgeable, conversational; not corporate/canned)
2. **Value-first:** Did you answer or offer value before asking for contact?
3. **No promises:** Did you avoid committing to timelines/outcomes you can't guarantee in public?
4. **Link included (if applicable):** Calendly link, property link, or lead magnet delivery link present?
5. **Response time:** Sent within 30-60 minutes for comments, <1 hour for DMs during business hours?
6. **Escalation routed (if needed):** Legal/compliance/threat questions flagged for Matt?
7. **Capture ready (if applicable):** If 2+ message DM thread completed, did you ask for email + phone + nurture sequence tag?
8. **Follow Up Boss tagged (if capture):** Contact added with correct keyword tag (strategy, buyer, moving, etc.)?

Mark complete only when verified. Incomplete responses signal to followers that community isn't being managed.

## Monthly Community Health Check

Run by agent, reported to Matt:

- **DM volume trend:** Up/down/flat week-over-week?
- **Response time average:** <30 min? >1 hour?
- **Capture rate:** What % of DMs convert to contacts?
- **Negative comment ratio:** Any uptick in complaints or trolls?
- **Review volume:** How many new reviews added this month?
- **Review response rate:** Responded to 100% of reviews?

If trend is declining (lower DM volume, slower responses, lower capture), investigate:
1. Content quality (hook/CTA weak?)
2. Timing (posting off-hours?)
3. Platform change (algorithm shift, check platform-algorithm-brief.md)
4. Response capacity (too many DMs to respond within 1 hour? Need support hiring)

## Community Management is Relationship Building

The goal is not to close sales fast. The goal is to build trust, serve first, and let leads naturally move into nurture sequences. Every response is a moment to show Matt's personality: authentic, knowledgeable, service-oriented, never salesy.

If a response sounds like it's pushing or pitching, rewrite it.
