## Instagram Algorithm Signals (April 2026)

**Ranked by weight (heaviest first):**

1. **Shares**, Exponentially more valuable than any other signal. Shares compound visibility across multiple networks (follower + recipient networks). One share = 3x the algorithmic boost of 10 likes.

2. **Direct Messages (DMs)**, Second most valuable signal. DM volume + speed determines reach multiplier. Posts with early DM activity get pushed to Explore first. This is why DM-CTA is mandatory on every post.

3. **Saves**, Intention signal (saved for later = high intent). Saved posts get re-ranked in followers' feeds. Equal weight to comments as of 2026.

4. **Comments**, Direct engagement but lower weight than saves. Comment depth (lengthy replies) weighted more than comment count.

5. **Watch time on Reels**, Duration watched + re-watches (if Reel replayed). Completion rate by first 3 seconds matters most.

6. **Likes**, Lowest weight signal. Engagement metric but doesn't move needle algorithmically. A post can have 1000 likes and zero reach if no shares/saves/DMs.

**Platform hierarchy:**
- Reels >> Feed posts >> Stories >> Carousels
- Reels dominate algorithmic reach (story/feed posts get 1/5 the algorithmic distribution as Reels)
- Carousels underweight vs Reels (even though carousel completion is high, algorithmic push is lower)

**Recency & Timing:**
- Recent post advantage exists for 48-72 hours (strong recency signal)
- Tuesday-Thursday 8am-4pm Bend time sees max audience availability
- Scheduling posts sacrifices ~20% reach vs instant posting (algorithm favors "freshness" + social timing)

**Cross-platform penalties (still active April 2026):**
- Watermarked content (TikTok watermark, YouTube watermark, IG watermark from repost) = downrank
- Cross-posted content from Facebook → Instagram = downrank (native IG content ranks 20% higher)
- Repetitive audio/hooks across multiple posts to same follower set = decreased reach on subsequent posts

**What DOESN'T work anymore (2026):**
- 30-hashtag dumps (algorithm penalizes hashtag spam; 5-10 niche hashtags only)
- Engagement bait ("Tag someone who," "Double-tap if," "Like if you agree") = shadowban risk
- Follow-for-follow exchanges = zero algorithmic value
- Asking followers to "share to their stories" via captions = does not boost reach

**Carousels note:**
Carousels (multi-slide posts) have higher user completion (people swipe through all slides) but lower algorithmic distribution weight than Reels. Use Reels for maximum reach; Carousels for storytelling depth when reach is secondary.

**Broadcast Channels & Trials:**
- Broadcast channels (segmented audience posts) don't compete in main feed; zero algorithmic disadvantage, but zero algorithmic advantage either
- Trials (24-48 hour limited posts) earn higher engagement ranking when enabled; use for time-sensitive content (property showings, limited offers)

## TikTok Algorithm Signals (April 2026)

**Ranked by weight:**

1. **Watch-through rate (WTR)**, King signal. Algorithm determines within first 3 seconds if video stays in user's for-you feed. 40%+ WTR by 30 min = push to broader audience. <30% WTR = feed death.

2. **Share rate**, More valuable than likes. Shares indicate "worth showing others" signal.

3. **Comment rate**, Engagement depth (comment length) weighted more than comment count.

4. **Like rate**, Least valuable engagement signal (lowest algorithmic weight).

5. **Rewatch rate**, If user watches video 2+ times, algorithm boosts distribution. Rewatchable content (humor, useful data, emotional) performs 2x.

6. **Click-through to profile**, Video driving profile clicks = audience building signal; boosts future video distribution.

7. **Text search**, On-screen text + spoken keywords in first 3 seconds indexed for TikTok search (TikTok is becoming a search engine, not just social app). Use keywords in caption, on-screen text, AND spoken.

**Platform hierarchy:**
- All content treated equally algorithmically (TikTok doesn't favor Shorts vs Long-form in ranking; watchtime does)
- Shorter videos (<45 sec) have higher WTR potential but lower total watchtime value
- 60-90 second videos hit sweet spot (high WTR potential + watchtime value for monetization)

**Recency & Longevity:**
- Videos earn views weeks/months after posting (unlike IG recency penalty)
- No scheduling penalty (TikTok doesn't care if post is instant or scheduled)
- Trending audio has 48-72 hour window; use trending sounds within that window for 3-5x WTR boost

**Watermarks kill reach:**
- Cross-platform watermarks (IG watermark, TikTok watermark from original creator, YouTube watermark) = instant downrank
- TikTok algorithm actively suppresses watermarked content (intentional feature to prevent plagiarism)
- Remove ALL watermarks before uploading

**Hashtag strategy:**
- 5-8 niche hashtags + 1-2 trending hashtags
- Hashtags less critical than IG (keywords in on-screen text matter more)
- Trending hashtags give 24-48 hour boost; use if relevant to content

**Comment response time:**
- Respond to comments within 24 hours (algorithm tracks this; high response rate = reach multiplier)
- Comment responses get pinned to top for 24 hours (drives visibility)

**What DOESN'T work anymore:**
- Trending audio misuse (using trending audio on unrelated content = downrank)
- Cross-posting watermarked content from IG = automatic reach suppression
- Engagement bait text ("Comment if you agree") = no algorithmic boost
- Hashtag spam (>15 hashtags) = zero value

## Facebook Algorithm Signals (April 2026)

**Ranked by weight:**

1. **Shares**, Most valuable signal. Shared posts reach both original followers AND recipients' networks.

2. **Comments with depth**, Lengthy comments + reply chains weighted more than comment count. Algorithm surfaces posts with discussion.

3. **Saves**, Secondary engagement signal (less weight than IG saves).

4. **Watch time on Reels**, Reels get algorithmic priority; watch completion in first 3 seconds matters.

5. **Likes**, Lowest weight (engagement but minimal reach impact).

6. **Click-through to external link**, Links in comment (not caption) still drive traffic without algorithmic penalty (change from 2024).

**Platform hierarchy:**
- Reels >> Feed posts (3-5x reach difference)
- Video content >> static image
- Native video > linked video

**Suggested content distribution:**
- 30% of Facebook reach goes to non-followers (algorithm serves Ryan Realty content to people outside current follower base)
- Suggested content = higher CPM, lower conversion (awareness-building, not direct sales)

**Cross-posting from Instagram:**
- Cross-posting from IG → FB no longer penalized (change from 2025)
- BUT native FB publishing outperforms shared posts by ~15%
- Best practice: publish native to both platforms

**Groups + Community:**
- Facebook Groups still have high engagement (algorithm favors Groups for discussion)
- Consider private community group for buyer/seller nurture (off-platform messaging, but FB Groups algorithm gives reach priority)

**What DOESN'T work anymore:**
- Clickbait headlines (algorithm suppresses low-engagement bait)
- Asking users to "like + comment" for engagement = no reach boost
- External link spam in captions = suppressed reach
- Repetitive content in quick succession = feed fatigue signal

## YouTube Shorts Algorithm (April 2026)

**Ranked by weight:**

1. **Swipe-away rate (first 2 seconds)**, Critical moment. If viewer swipes away in first 2 seconds, video tanks algorithmically. Hook must be visual + immediate.

2. **Watch time**, Total duration watched before swipe (average watch time is primary metric).

3. **Rewatches**, If viewer watches Shorts 2+ times, exponential reach boost.

4. **Share rate**, Shares drive algorithm distribution.

5. **Comment rate**, Lower weight than Long-form but still signals.

**Title importance (different from Reels):**
- Title is keyword-first query (e.g., "Acreage Homes Under $1.5M in Bend Oregon" not "Check this out")
- Title CTR (click-through from YouTube search/suggested) is ranking signal
- Optimize for search intent, not curiosity gap (different from IG)

**Long-form vs Shorts:**
- Long-form videos (5-15 min) prioritized for watch-time based monetization
- Shorts prioritized for subscription growth (subscribers feed Long-form videos)
- Best strategy: Use Shorts to drive subscriber growth; use Long-form for watchtime revenue

**What DOESN'T work:**
- Clickbait titles (YouTube ranks on CTR but punishes false promise; viewers bounce = algorithm penalty)
- Watermarked content = reach suppression
- No description/keyword info = lost search opportunity

## LinkedIn Algorithm Signals (April 2026)

**Ranked by weight:**

1. **Dwell time**, Time spent reading post (algorithm measures how long user stays on post). Longer posts that engage readers = higher ranking. This is why 200+ word posts outperform short posts.

2. **Comments**, Comments > likes. Algorithm weights discussion depth heavily. Long comment threads = exponential reach boost.

3. **Shares**, Moderately high weight (less than dwell time but more than likes).

4. **Reactions (likes)**, Lowest weight. Engagement metric but minimal ranking impact.

5. **Click-through on links**, Outbound links still carry slight penalty (LinkedIn favors native content over external traffic). Use sparingly.

**Personal vs Company page:**
- Personal profiles earn 8-10x more reach than company pages (as of April 2026)
- Strategy: Publish on personal profile (Matt Ryan), secondary cross-post to company page
- Personal followers are more engaged than company page followers

**Native content priority:**
- Native text posts > link posts (LinkedIn favors keeping users on platform)
- Native video > link to external video

**Engagement signals specific to LinkedIn:**
- Reaction type matters (Thoughtful reaction > Like)
- Comment from connection with many followers = boosted visibility
- Algorithm tracks "who commented" (influencer comments increase reach)

**What DOESN'T work:**
- Salesy language (LinkedIn suppresses overtly promotional content)
- Multi-link posts (algorithm deprioritizes external link spam)
- Hashtag dumping (hashtags used sparingly on LinkedIn; less critical than IG/TikTok)
- Generic engagement bait = suppressed reach

## Google Business Profile Algorithm (April 2026)

**Ranking factors for local pack (top 3 results):**

1. **Review velocity + volume**, Number of new reviews + response rate to reviews is #1 local-pack signal. One new review per week > inactive profile.

2. **Review quality + recency**, Recent reviews weighted more than older reviews. High-star reviews boost ranking; low-star reviews with Matt's response can recover ranking.

3. **Review response rate**, Response to 100% of reviews = local-pack boost. Response within 24 hours > slower responses.

4. **"What's New" post frequency**, Weekly posts signal active business. Inactive profiles (no "What's New" posts) = local-pack demotion.

5. **Q&A completeness**, Detailed Q&A answers + FAQ coverage = local-pack signal.

6. **Post engagement**, Clicks on "What's New" posts (phone calls, direction clicks, website clicks) signal relevance.

7. **Business info completeness**, All fields filled (hours, address, phone, website, photos) = baseline requirement.

**Review response strategy:**
- Response within 24 hours > later response (recency signal)
- Response to negative reviews most important (Google rewards recovery reputation management)
- Response length: 1-2 sentences sufficient (quality > length for local-pack)

**"What's New" post strategy:**
- Minimum: 1 per week (algorithm signal for active business)
- Optimal: 2-3 per week (property listings, market tips, events)
- Ideal: 4+ per week for competitive markets (Bend market is moderately competitive)

**AI-generated responses (as of Nov 2025):**
- Google allows AI-generated Q&A responses on FAQ-style questions
- Requires manual review before publishing (not auto-post eligible)
- Use for common questions (mortgage, timeline, buyer requirements)

## Universal Rules (All Platforms)

**What works everywhere:**
1. Hook in first 3 seconds/words (platform universal)
2. Strong call-to-action appropriate to platform (DM on IG, comments on LinkedIn, shares on TikTok)
3. Native publishing over cross-posting (native always outperforms cross-post by 15-20%)
4. Consistency > volume (4 posts/week every week > 10 posts then silence)
5. Response speed within 1 hour (algorithm signal on most platforms)

**What kills reach everywhere:**
1. Watermarked cross-platform content (instant downrank)
2. Engagement bait ("tag someone," "like if," "share to win") = suppression
3. Misleading headlines/hooks = bounce = downrank
4. Hashtag spam (platform-specific counts; 30 hashtags on IG = penalty)
5. Repetitive content to same audience within 48 hours = feed fatigue signal
