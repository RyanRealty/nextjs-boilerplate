# Ryan Realty: Trending Audio Research Skill

## When to Use
Use this skill every Monday morning (or before starting a new content batch) to identify trending audio for Instagram Reels, TikTok, and Shorts. Audio choice directly impacts algorithm distribution and watch-through rates.

## Before Starting
Read `Organic_Growth_Intelligence.md` (BRAND MANAGER folder) for platform algorithm mechanics and audio impact on reach.

---

## Where to Find Trending Audio

### TikTok Creative Center (Tier 1 Source)
**URL**: https://www.tiktok.com/creator/discovery (requires Matt's TikTok Pro account)

**What you see**:
- Trending Sounds ranked by usage velocity (rising, peaking, declining).
- 14-day trends with usage metrics.
- Category filters (Dance, Comedy, Education, Lifestyle, Music).

**Action**: Pull the top 10 sounds from the "Rising" and "Peaking" categories. Note the track title, creator, and usage count. Take screenshots for reference.

**Time commitment**: 10 minutes.

### Instagram Reels Audio Library ("Audio Explosion" Icon)
**How to access**: Open a blank Reel in Instagram Creator Studio. Tap the "Audio" icon in the bottom navigation. Scroll through "Trending Now" and "Recommended For You" sections.

**What you see**:
- Top trending sounds in your region (Bend, Oregon).
- Usage count and momentum indicators.
- Commercial-use flags for Pages (critical for Ryan Realty account).

**Action**: Filter by "Pages approved" (Matt's is a business account, so you have access to a different library than personal accounts). Grab 5-7 trending options. Screenshot the audio titles and note the usage count.

**Critical rule**: TikTok and Instagram audio libraries are different. Never assume a sound trending on TikTok will trend on Instagram. Verify both independently.

**Time commitment**: 12 minutes.

### Instagram Trend Insights (If Available)
**Check availability**: In Creator Studio, go to Insights > Discovery. Look for "Audio Trends" or "Trending Audio by Region".

**What you see** (if available): Audio ranked by region-specific momentum. Bend/Oregon market data if available.

**Note**: This feature is not available in all accounts or regions. If missing, rely on TikTok Creative Center + IG Audio Library instead.

**Time commitment**: 5 minutes if feature exists.

### Third-Party Tools (API-Ish Access)
If you need deeper trend data or historical momentum tracking:

**Predis.io** (paid, but has free trial)
- Upload a TikTok video URL. Tool returns trending sounds, hashtags, and performance predictions.
- Useful for competitive audio research (what are other Bend real estate creators using?).

**Tokboard.com** (free tier exists)
- Trending sounds filtered by category, region, usage over time.
- Solid backup if TikTok Creative Center is slow.

**Tokchart.com** (free for basic trends)
- Trending sounds with velocity arrows (rising vs. peaking).
- Simple interface, good for quick weekly scans.

**Action**: Use one of these as a backup if primary sources lag. Log the data in your audio log (see below).

**Time commitment**: 15 minutes if using third-party tools.

## Audio Selection Criteria

Not every trending sound works for Ryan Realty. Use this filter to rank candidates:

### Momentum Direction (Rank 1-10)
- **Rising** (8-10 points): Early in trend cycle, room to ride the wave without sounding late.
- **Peaking** (5-7 points): Maximum visibility now, but may drop next week.
- **Declining** (1-3 points): Skip. Algorithm gives older sounds less reach.

### Genre Fit for Ryan Realty (Rank 1-10)
Ryan Realty default audio personality: cinematic-warm, folk-acoustic, low-fi chill, orchestral swell, acoustic guitar, analog warm.

**Good fit** (8-10 points):
- Indie folk, acoustic guitar, warm lo-fi beats
- Orchestral swells (drama without bombast)
- Ambient, nature sounds + music overlay
- Acoustic indie pop (warm vocals, guitar-driven)
- Soft synth, minimal electronic (not trap or EDM)

**Neutral fit** (5-7 points):
- Pop hits with mass appeal (they work but are generic)
- Upbeat indie (okay if the vibe is not too hyper)
- Chill hip hop beats

**Poor fit** (1-4 points):
- Trap, bass-heavy beats (contradicts brand tone)
- Overly comedic or TikTok-meme sounds (not real estate appropriate)
- Harsh synths, aggressive energy
- Sounds that feel "salesy" (high-energy hype)

### Commercial Use Flag (Pages Account Rules)
**Critical**: Matt's Instagram is a Pages/Business account. Meta has restricted the music library available to business accounts (licensing restrictions).

**Rule**: Always verify the audio has a green checkmark or "commercial use approved" label in IG's audio library. If TikTok has a sound but IG doesn't offer it for Pages, find an alternative.

**What to do if a sound is TikTok-trending but not IG-approved**: Note it for TikTok-only content, but don't force it into IG Reels. Substitute with the next-best match from IG's library.

## Weekly Audio Research Workflow

**Monday 8am Bend time: Scout Phase**

1. Open TikTok Creative Center. Pull top 10 Rising + Peaking sounds. Note trend direction, track title, creator.
2. Open IG audio library. Scroll Trending Now. Note 5-7 candidates with Pages approval.
3. If time: Check Tokboard or Predis for validation/overlap.
4. Create a spreadsheet or doc with candidates:
   ```
   | Date Scouted | Platform | Audio Title | Creator | Direction | Fit Score | Pages Approved? | Notes |
   |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
   | 2026-04-15 | TikTok | "Summer Breeze" | indie artist | Rising | 8 | N/A | Good alt
   | 2026-04-15 | IG | "Golden Hour" | stock audio | Peaking | 9 | YES | Best fit this week
   ```

**Monday 10am: Ranking Phase**

1. Score each candidate (Momentum + Genre Fit + Commercial Use = Total Score).
2. Pick your top 3 candidates.
3. Save them to a "Ready to Post" note or folder in your audio log.

**During the week: Assignment Phase**

As content is being created, assign audio from the "Ready to Post" list. Only swap if a content piece demands something different (e.g., educational voiceover content may need no music, just voiceover).

**End of week: Archive Phase**

Log which audio was actually used. Tag as "posted" or "skipped". Update momentum (did the peaking sound already decline?).

## Real Estate Audio Rules (Ryan Realty Specific)

### Prefer Original Sound Over Music
If a real estate creator (your tier, market, or niche) has posted a Reel with an original sound (voiceover, narration, or beat they created), that original track is often safer to reuse than licensed music. Fewer copyright claims, lower false-strike risk for Pages accounts.

**Action**: When you find a great real estate hook audio from another agent or creator, check if it's an "Original Sound" (marked in IG/TikTok). If so, save it as a backup option.

### Avoid Hyped Meme Sounds
TikTok's trending audio list includes lots of meme-adjacent sounds that peak fast and become associated with trends, not quality. Skip these for real estate content. They date content instantly.

### Short Audio Over Long Tracks
Reels perform best when the audio matches the content length exactly (no silence at the end, no cutting off mid-phrase). If a trending sound is 45 seconds and your Reel is 30, don't force it.

**Better approach**: Find a 28-32 second version of the same vibe, or pick different audio.

### Avoid "Salesy" Energy
Even if a sound is trending, if it has high-energy hype, urgency, or sales-pitch energy, it will contradict Ryan Realty voice. Example: aggressive motivational music with "hustle" lyrics. Reject it even if rising.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use the same audio on both TikTok and IG?**
A: Yes, if the audio exists on both platforms. But re-edit the Reel for each platform (remove IG watermark on TikTok version, adjust captions per platform norms).

**Q: What if an audio is trending on TikTok but not available on IG?**
A: Post on TikTok, find the closest equivalent for IG (same vibe, different track). Do not post a TikTok video to IG unchanged, algorithm will suppress it.

**Q: How many audio candidates should I have "in inventory" at any time?**
A: Minimum 5-7 in the "Ready to Post" list so you have options without re-scouting mid-week.

**Q: Can I reuse audio from last month if it still feels good?**
A: No. Algorithm penalizes older audio. Viewers skip past it. Always refresh weekly.

**Q: What about silence or voiceover-only content?**
A: Valid for educational reels (process videos, explainers). But Reels with audio outperform silent content by 2-3x. Use silence only if voiceover is essential.
