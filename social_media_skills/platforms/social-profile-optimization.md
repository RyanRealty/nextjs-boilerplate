## 0. Critical Context: Brokerage Account, Not Personal Agent

**All Ryan Realty social profiles represent the brokerage, not an individual agent.** Matt is the owner and principal broker, but he does not maintain a separate personal broker account. Every bio, every profile, every platform is Ryan Realty the company.

### What this changes
- Name fields use "Ryan Realty" not "Matt Ryan | Bend Realtor"
- The voice is still Matt's (he IS the brokerage) but framed as the company
- Profile photos: logo or Matt's headshot as the face of the brokerage (Matt's call)
- "We" is acceptable. "I" works too because Matt is the voice. Either is fine as long as it's consistent within a single platform.
- Credentials like "Principal Broker" can appear in supporting text but never lead
- The brokerage positioning is community-first, data-forward, no-BS real estate in Bend
- Category labels should be "Real Estate Agent" or "Real Estate Company" depending on what each platform offers for business accounts

### What this does NOT change
- The 6 patterns still apply (positioning over resume, personality over service list, etc.)
- All copy rules still apply
- All banned phrases still apply
- Matt's authentic voice is still the voice. Ryan Realty sounds like Matt because Matt is Ryan Realty.

## 2. The 6 Patterns (from 500K+ follower agents)

These are empirical, not opinions. Every top performer follows them. Serhant, Baker, Simon, Jordan, Londono, Caballero, Lindahl, Nartey all share these traits.

### Pattern 1: Positioning statement, not resume
Lead with a point of view. Not what you do. What you believe, what you stand for, or how you see the world. Serhant's bio is 8 words. Cash Jordan's is 4. None of them open with "Licensed broker with 15 years of experience."

### Pattern 2: Credentials buried or absent
Professional qualifications do not appear in the bio. Ever. If they show up at all, they're in a secondary "about" section or a pinned post. The bio is for personality.

### Pattern 3: Personality IS the content
The agent's authentic voice is the differentiator. Glennda Baker doesn't sell houses in her bio. She's Glennda. The Broke Agent doesn't mention closings. He's funny about the industry. The brand is the person, not the service.

### Pattern 4: One CTA maximum
Not "DM me AND call me AND visit my website AND check my listings." One action. The best bios often have zero explicit CTAs because the content itself drives the action.

### Pattern 5: Minimal emoji use
Functional only. A pin emoji before a location, a link emoji before a URL. Never decorative. Never a row of house/key/sparkle emojis. Emoji clutter signals amateur.

### Pattern 6: Zero agency language
The following phrases appear in exactly zero top-performer bios:
- "Boutique brokerage"
- "White-glove service"
- "Unparalleled expertise"
- "Seamless experience"
- "Your trusted [anything]"
- "No franchise script"
- "Exceptional customer service"
- "Full-service"
- "Dedicated to"
- "Committed to excellence"
- "Passionate about"

These are kill phrases. If any of them appear in a draft, the draft is wrong.

## 3. Matt's Voice (What It Actually Sounds Like)

### The brand voice in one sentence
Smart, funny, knows the data, doesn't take himself too seriously, treats every client like a neighbor.

### Two modes (from feedback_social_humor_tone.md)

**Client-facing** (emails, DMs, consults, listing descriptions): Honest, transparent, professional. Warm but restrained humor. The 9 brand voice characteristics at full strength.

**Social content** (bios, Reels, TikTok, posts, Stories): All 9 characteristics PLUS humor, personality, meme energy, data-nerd swagger. This is where we show that real estate can be fun.

### Profile bios fall in between
A bio is public-facing but it's also a first impression. It should read like Matt talking to someone at a Bend brewery, not like a brochure. Closer to social mode than client mode, but without the meme energy. Think: confident, warm, a little funny, zero bullshit.

### Voice litmus tests (from Brand Voice & Tone Guide)
1. Would I say this face-to-face at a coffee shop?
2. Does this sound like a real person or a marketing department?
3. Am I telling, or am I showing?
4. Would this make someone feel something, or just scroll past?

If any answer is wrong, rewrite.

## 4. Copy Rules (Enforced on Every Bio)

These come from `feedback_copy_writing_rules.md` and are non-negotiable.

- **No em dashes.** Not anywhere. Not as a stylistic break.
- **No hyphens in prose.** "4 bedroom" not "4-bedroom."
- **No setup or preamble lines.** Don't write "I wanted to give you a real picture" or any variation.
- **Never state what the reader already knows.** Don't open with "As a Central Oregon homebuyer" or any variant.
- **No salesy language.** No "worth a serious look." No "must-see." No pressure framing.
- **No editorializing.** Don't add commentary or spin. Let the content speak.
- **No pandering.** Don't tell the audience what they want to hear.
- **Never overtly state the tone.** Don't write "you can trust us" or "we're here for you" or "we're the experts." Telling someone you're trustworthy is the opposite of trustworthy.
- **No comma-heavy feature lists.** Write in clear short sentences.

## 5. Bio Formulas by Platform

### Instagram (150 char limit)

**Structure:**
```
[Positioning line — who Matt is, point of view]
[Location anchor — Bend specific]
[One CTA or value prop]
```

**Rules:**
- 150 characters hard limit. Every character counts.
- Line breaks work. Use them to create visual breathing room.
- One emoji max (functional only, like a pin for location).
- No hashtags in bio (waste of characters, not indexed for search anymore).
- The link field handles URLs (up to 5 native links). Don't waste bio space on "link in bio."
- Name field is searchable. Use keywords: "Ryan Realty | Bend, Oregon" (26 chars, under 30 limit).

**What NOT to do:**
```
❌ 🏡 Your trusted Central Oregon brokerage | Committed to building community
   through authentic relationships and exceptional customer service ✨
```
This is what Matt currently has. Every word of it is wrong.

### TikTok (80 char limit)

**Structure:**
```
[Hook — personality or POV in under 80 chars]
```

**Rules:**
- 80 characters. That's it. Brutally short.
- This is the most personality-forward platform. Lead with humor or a hot take.
- No credentials. No brokerage name. No phone number.
- Link goes in the separate URL field (requires 1K followers or business account).
- Name field: searchable, 30 chars max. Use "Ryan Realty | Bend Oregon" for discoverability.

### YouTube (1,000 char "About" section)

**Structure:**
```
[Opening line — what the channel is about, what viewers get]
[2-3 sentences on the content: market data, Bend lifestyle, real estate education]
[One line on who Matt is — minimal, personality-forward]
[CTA — subscribe reason or link]
```

**Rules:**
- First 100-150 chars show in search results. Front-load value.
- YouTube is a search engine. Use natural keywords: "Bend Oregon real estate," "Central Oregon housing market," "buying a home in Bend."
- Don't keyword-stuff. Write for humans first.
- Channel name and handle matter for SEO. "Ryan Realty | Bend Oregon Real Estate" or similar.
- Banner safe zone: 1546x423 px (the rest is cropped on various devices).

### LinkedIn (2,600 char "About" section)

**Structure:**
```
[Opening line — professional positioning, not resume]
[What Matt brings: market knowledge, data-first approach, community orientation]
[Brief Bend/Central Oregon context]
[How to connect]
```

**Rules:**
- LinkedIn bios can be longer but the first 2-3 lines show before "see more." Hook fast.
- Professional but not corporate. Still sounds like Matt, not a press release.
- This is the ONE platform where a line about experience is acceptable (not leading, not first line, stated casually).
- Headline field (220 chars): "Principal Broker, Ryan Realty | Bend, Oregon" or a positioning variant.
- No "serial entrepreneur." No "thought leader." No "helping families achieve their dreams."

### Facebook Page (255 char "About" / "Additional Information" is longer)

**Structure:**
```
[Short About — 1-2 lines, personality-forward, location-anchored]
[Additional Info — slightly more detail, still voice-compliant]
```

**Rules:**
- "About" truncates early in most views. Keep the best stuff up front.
- Facebook skews slightly older. Tone can be a touch warmer. Still no clichés.
- CTA should point to Messenger or the website, not a phone number (FB users message, they don't call).
- Category: "Real Estate Agent" (not "Real Estate Company").

### Google Business Profile (750 char description)

**Structure:**
```
[What Ryan Realty does — stated plainly, location keywords natural]
[What makes it different — without saying "what makes us different"]
[Service area specifics — Bend, Redmond, Sisters, Central Oregon]
```

**Rules:**
- GBP is for search. Keywords matter more here than any other platform.
- But Google penalizes keyword stuffing. Write natural sentences that happen to include "Bend Oregon real estate," "Central Oregon homes," "Deschutes County."
- No ALL CAPS. No exclamation marks. No "CALL NOW."
- Hours, address, phone must be accurate and match every other platform (NAP consistency).
- This is the most "informational" bio. Less personality, more utility. But still no agency clichés.

### Zillow / Realtor.com / Redfin (varies, typically 500-2000 chars)

**Structure:**
```
[Opening — what buyers/sellers can expect working with Matt]
[Market expertise — Bend-specific knowledge, data literacy]
[Brief personal touch — Bend resident, community involvement]
```

**Rules:**
- Portal bios are read by people actively searching for an agent. They're further down the funnel.
- Slightly more detail is OK here. Still no resume format.
- Include Bend neighborhoods and areas served naturally in the text.
- Social proof (if available) works here: closed transactions, years in market.
- No superlatives. No "top agent." No "#1 in Bend." Unless it's a verifiable, specific stat.

## 6. Keyword Strategy by Platform

### Instagram / TikTok
- Name field is the only searchable text. Pack it: "Matt Ryan | Bend Realtor"
- Bio text is NOT indexed for search. Write for humans, not algorithms.
- Hashtags in bio are not indexed (changed in 2023). Don't use them.

### YouTube
- Channel name, description, and video titles are all indexed.
- Target: "Bend Oregon real estate," "Central Oregon housing market," "Bend homes for sale," "moving to Bend Oregon"
- Long-tail: "best neighborhoods in Bend," "Bend real estate market update [year]"

### LinkedIn
- Headline, About, and Experience sections are indexed by LinkedIn search.
- Target: "Principal Broker," "Bend Oregon," "Central Oregon real estate," "Ryan Realty"

### Google Business Profile
- Business name, description, categories, and reviews are all indexed.
- Primary category: "Real Estate Agent"
- Secondary categories: "Real Estate Agency" (if applicable)
- Description keywords: "Bend," "Central Oregon," "Deschutes County," "Redmond," "Sisters," "Sunriver," "real estate," "homes for sale," "buying," "selling"

### Portals (Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin)
- Agent bio text is indexed within the portal's search.
- Neighborhood names matter: Old Bend, NorthWest Crossing, Tetherow, Broken Top, River West, Summit West, Awbrey Butte, Shevlin, Mountain High, Southeast Bend
- Service type keywords: "buyer's agent," "listing agent," "first-time homebuyer," "luxury homes," "investment properties," "relocation"

## 7. NAP Consistency (Non-Negotiable)

**NAP = Name, Address, Phone.** Must match exactly on every platform.

**Canonical values:**
- **Name:** Ryan Realty
- **Address:** 115 NW Oregon Ave Suite #2, Bend, OR 97703
- **Phone:** 541.213.6706 (this format everywhere, per brand guide)
- **Email:** matt@ryan-realty.com

**Why this matters:** Whitespark research found 27% drop in local search visibility from NAP inconsistency. Google cross-references every listing. One wrong address undermines all the others.

**Check on every platform update:** Before changing any profile, verify NAP matches canonical values.

## 9. Profile Photo Strategy

**Primary headshot:** Professional headshot on white background (from Google Drive > 06_Marketing & Brand > Headshots). Use the SAME photo on every single platform. Not different crops. Not different photos. The same image, resized per platform specs.

**Why:** Visual recognition across platforms. Someone who follows on Instagram should recognize the same face on Zillow instantly.

**Specs per platform (from social-channel-specs.md):**
- Instagram: 1080x1080 upload, displayed 110x110 mobile / 180x180 desktop (circle crop)
- TikTok: 200x200 minimum (circle crop)
- YouTube: 800x800 recommended (circle crop)
- Facebook: 720x720 minimum (circle crop)
- LinkedIn: 400x400 minimum (circle crop)
- GBP: 720x720 recommended (circle crop)
- Portals: varies, usually 300x300+ square

**Action:** Export one master headshot at 1080x1080 px, then resize for each platform. Circle crop means keep face centered with margin.

## 10. Quality Gate (Run Before Shipping Any Bio)

Every bio must pass ALL of these checks before it goes live:

### Voice checks
- [ ] Would Matt say this face-to-face at a Bend brewery?
- [ ] Does it sound like a real person or a marketing department?
- [ ] Zero kill phrases (Section 2, Pattern 6 list)?
- [ ] Zero em dashes?
- [ ] Zero hyphens in prose?
- [ ] Zero preamble/setup lines?
- [ ] Zero statements of what the reader already knows?
- [ ] Zero salesy language?
- [ ] Zero editorializing?
- [ ] Zero overt tone statements ("you can trust us")?

### Structure checks
- [ ] Follows the 6 patterns?
- [ ] Leads with positioning, not credentials?
- [ ] One CTA or fewer?
- [ ] Minimal emoji (functional only)?
- [ ] Under character limit for the platform?
- [ ] NAP values match canonical (Section 7)?

### Platform checks
- [ ] Name/display field includes searchable keywords?
- [ ] Handle is @ryanrealtybend (or platform equivalent)?
- [ ] Profile photo is the approved headshot?
- [ ] Contact fields are filled correctly?
- [ ] Link field points to ryan-realty.com?

### Litmus test
Read the bio out loud. If it sounds like it could be on any real estate agent's profile in any city, it's wrong. It should sound like it could only come from Matt Ryan in Bend, Oregon.

## 12. Process for Writing New Bios

1. **Pick the platform.** Read its specific formula (Section 5).
2. **Draft in Matt's voice.** Imagine him at 10 Barrel, explaining what he does to someone who just moved to Bend. Write that.
3. **Run the quality gate** (Section 10). Every checkbox.
4. **Run brand-voice:brand-voice-enforcement** on the final draft.
5. **Check character count** against platform limit.
6. **Verify NAP consistency** if the bio includes contact info.
7. **Present to Matt for approval.** One platform at a time or a batch, but never ship without sign-off.
