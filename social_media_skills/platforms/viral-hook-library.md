# Ryan Realty: Viral Hook Library Skill

## When to Use
Use this skill when creating opening lines or first-frame content for Instagram Reels, TikTok, or Facebook videos. Every hook must stop the scroll in the first second and hold retention through final frame.

## Before Starting
Read `Organic_Growth_Intelligence.md` (BRAND MANAGER folder) for algorithm mechanics and hook performance benchmarks.

---

## Hook Architecture: The Retention Beats

Every Reel/TikTok must hit these moments or lose the viewer:

**Second 0-1: The Hook**
- The opening line or visual must create immediate pattern interrupt or curiosity gap.
- If it doesn't make someone pause, everything else fails.
- Rule: First words on-screen must be the most compelling statement in the entire video.

**Second 1-3: The Tease**
- Confirm the hook with a follow-up statement or visual that deepens curiosity.
- The viewer is deciding now whether to keep watching.
- This is where you plant the promise of payoff.

**Second 3-7: The Value/Tension**
- Build stakes or introduce conflict.
- Show why this matters to the viewer's life or decision-making.
- Use visuals or data to anchor the narrative.

**Second 7-15: The Development**
- Expand the core idea with supporting details, examples, or proof.
- Keep the pace moving (no static shots longer than 3 seconds).
- Use cut rate and pacing to signal importance.

**Second 15-End: The Resolution**
- Deliver the payoff or call to action.
- For listing content: reveal price, neighborhood, acreage. For education: state the takeaway. For market data: show the implication.
- Last frame must hold for 1 second with on-screen text that reinforces the CTA.

## Ryan Realty Hook Categories (On-Brand Only)

Every hook belongs to one of these five categories. Anything else is off-brand or salesy.

### 1. Bend Market Stat Shock
Hook the viewer with a specific, recent number that creates emotion or surprise.

**Why it works**: Numbers are trustworthy and create a "wait, what?" reaction.

**Examples**:
- "Bend home prices jumped 8 percent this month alone."
- "The average days-on-market just hit 47. That's the slowest in two years."
- "Three neighborhoods just crossed the 1-million-dollar threshold."
- "Inventory is up 23 percent. Here's what that means for your offer."
- "We've closed 12 sales over 2 million this quarter."

**How to execute**: Lead with the number in all caps or as a bold visual reveal. Always cite the source (listings data, MLS, market report). End with "DM me 'market'" for non-listing, or "DM me 'tour'" if promoting a specific property in that market.

### 2. Buyer/Seller Myth Break
Hook by contradicting a common belief or misconception that the viewer likely holds.

**Why it works**: Contrarian claims are high-retention hooks. Viewers keep watching to see if you're right.

**Examples**:
- "No, you don't need 20 percent down. Here's what you actually need."
- "Most agents price listings too high. Here's the math that proves it."
- "Your credit score matters less than you think. Here's why."
- "The spring market doesn't guarantee higher prices. Here's the data."
- "You don't need a traditional loan to buy in Bend anymore."

**How to execute**: State the myth clearly (what people believe), then immediately address it (what's actually true). Use visuals or voiceover to walk through the evidence. End with "DM me 'guide'" or "DM me 'strategy'".

### 3. Neighborhood Spotlight Reveal
Hook by framing a specific Bend neighborhood as the answer to an unspoken question: "Where should I live?"

**Why it works**: People are searching for community. Showing them a place through professional video activates decision-making.

**Examples**:
- "This neighborhood has trails, breweries, and homes under 500k. Where is it?"
- "Bend's most walkable neighborhood just had three homes hit the market."
- "If you want mountain views without the price tag, this is your neighborhood."
- "This area has the best schools and the shortest commute. Here's why."
- "Quietest neighborhood in Bend. Proof in the next 30 seconds."

**How to execute**: Use Snowdrift Visuals footage (photo + video folders in Drive). Open with the neighborhood name or a signature visual. Walk through 2-3 differentiators (schools, trails, restaurants, price point). End with "DM me 'Bend'" or address of a listing in that area.

### 4. Historic Bend Story Hook
Hook by telling a true, grounded story about why Bend exists or what makes it distinctive. Avoid tourist-y language.

**Why it works**: People connect to narrative and authenticity. This separates Ryan Realty from national brands.

**Examples**:
- "Bend started as a logging town. Now it's the outdoor capital of the Pacific Northwest. Here's how we got here."
- "This neighborhood was built in the 1970s when Bend was still a secret. Now everyone knows about it."
- "The Three Sisters Wilderness shaped everything about how Bend was built. Here's the proof."
- "Bend's original downtown was a ghost town in 1990. Then this happened."
- "Three volcanic peaks define Bend. Here's what living near them actually feels like."

**How to execute**: Open with a historical fact or image. Show the "then" (archive or period photo) and "now" (current neighborhood or listing footage). Keep voiceover conversational and grounded. End with "DM me 'story'" or a call to explore a specific neighborhood.

### 5. Process Demystification
Hook by answering a question that buyers or sellers actually ask but agents often explain poorly.

**Why it works**: Education builds trust. People follow agents who teach, not agents who sell.

**Examples**:
- "Here's exactly what happens between your offer and closing. No fluff."
- "Your home inspection report just came back. Now what?"
- "You just got pre-approved. Here's what that actually means."
- "Appraisal came in low. Here's your next move."
- "This is what my job actually is. Not what you think."

**How to execute**: State the process or question as the hook. Use on-screen text, voiceover, or visuals to walk through 3-5 steps. Be specific and avoid jargon. End with "DM me 'explain'" or "DM me 'guide'".

## Forbidden Hook Patterns (Never Use These)

These patterns violate Ryan Realty brand voice and algorithm best practices:

- "Calling all homebuyers" / "Attention, buyers"
- "Your dream home awaits"
- "You won't believe what I found"
- "Tag someone who wants to buy" / "Tag your Bend bestie"
- "Don't miss out on this opportunity"
- "DM me NOW" (urgency-baiting kills algorithm)
- Any hook that sounds like a real estate ad
- "Swipe to see the rest" without the hook standing alone
- Engagement-bait ("Like this if you agree")

## DM CTA Enforcement

Every post must end with a DM keyword trigger. This is algorithm fuel, not a sales tactic.

**Required format**: "DM me '[keyword]'" on last frame or as final spoken line.

**Approved keywords** (by pillar):
- Market/Education content: 'market', 'strategy', 'guide', 'explain', 'tips'
- Listing content: 'tour', 'showing', 'walk'
- Neighborhood content: 'Bend', '[neighborhood name]', 'area'
- Personality/story content: 'story', 'interview', 'chat'

**Why it works**: Instagram ranks DM shares as the #1 engagement signal. Users DM the keyword to friends or themselves. That signals high-intent interaction and the algorithm amplifies it.

## Hook Testing Framework

Never ship a single hook variant. Test three, measure, scale the winner.

**Weekly testing cadence**:
1. Write three hook variants for the same topic (same script, different openings).
2. Post all three to Instagram Trials (or across different days if Trials unavailable).
3. Track watch-through %, shares, saves, DMs per 100 views.
4. The winner becomes your template for variations on that topic.
5. Document the winner in the hooks bank below.

**What to measure**:
- Watch-through % (hold-rate at 1s, 3s, final frame)
- DM click rate
- Save and share rate
- Comments (engagement type tells you if viewers understood the hook)

**Scaling a winner**:
Once a hook proves high-retention, create 5-7 variations on the same core idea and post across a two-week window.
