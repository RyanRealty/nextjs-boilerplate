# Friction Topics — RE Humor Taxonomy

The library of real estate friction points that consistently land as memes. Each entry maps to (a) the audience that gets it, (b) the templates that fit, (c) the data anchor it usually wants, and (d) compliance landmines to watch for.

When the trend scan returns a new candidate, find the closest entry below. If nothing fits, do not ship a stretch — the friction has to be real.

---

## Rate cycle

**Friction:** "I'll wait for rates to drop" → buyer waits 18 months while monthly payment stays the same because the price compensates.

**Audience:** Both. Agents have lived this reel for three years. Consumers are the protagonist.

**Best templates:** `pull_quote_card`, `drake_yes_no`, `nobody_me_at_3am`, `expectation_vs_reality`

**Data anchor:** 30-day rate change + 30-day price change + monthly-payment delta on a representative Bend median home.

**Compliance:** The forecast trap. Never say "rates will drop" or "rates will spike". Frame as "this is what the math looks like today" with a citation.

**Voice calibration (real lines from real accounts to read first):**
- The Broke Agent: "Rates dropped, prices went up, your payment is the same. Welcome to real estate."
- (verify any quote against the live account before using)

## Inventory tension

**Friction:** Buyers wait for "more inventory." Sellers wait for "the right buyer." Neither party reads the months-of-supply chart.

**Audience:** Both, agent-skewing.

**Best templates:** `distracted_boyfriend`, `pov_youre_a`, `pull_quote_card`

**Data anchor:** Active listings, sold last 30 days, months of supply, DOM. Bend-specific.

**Compliance:** None major. Stay on the math.

---

## "Just looking" buyers

**Friction:** Buyer says "just looking", writes an offer 4 days later. Or the inverse: buyer says "ready to write today", ghosts after the showing.

**Audience:** Agent-coded, but consumers recognize themselves.

**Best templates:** `imessage_screenshot`, `nobody_me_at_3am`, `tell_me_without_telling_me`

**Data anchor:** None usually. Observational.

**Compliance:** Use composite buyer characters. Never make a real client identifiable.

---

## Lowball offers

**Friction:** Listed at $850K. Offer at $620K with the line "we feel this is fair given the market."

**Audience:** Both. Sellers love seeing the absurdity. Buyers see themselves and laugh anyway.

**Best templates:** `pull_quote_card`, `imessage_screenshot`, `drake_yes_no`

**Data anchor:** Optional. Could cite Bend median sale-to-list ratio.

**Compliance:** Use composite numbers. Never quote a real offer on a real listing.

---

## Seller delusion

**Friction:** Seller insists their house is worth $200K more than the comps. "We put granite in 2007."

**Audience:** Both. Agents nod, consumers laugh at the type.

**Best templates:** `expectation_vs_reality`, `pull_quote_card`, `drake_yes_no`

**Data anchor:** Optional. Comps math works here.

**Compliance:** The joke must be on the *expectation*, not the *person*. Do not punch down.

---

## Showing logistics

**Friction:** Lockbox code wrong. Neighbor showing up to gossip mid-tour. Kid spilling juice on the white couch. The sprinkler going off as you walk up.

**Audience:** Agent-heavy, but the chaos is universal.

**Best templates:** `pov_youre_a`, `this_is_fine_dog`, `imessage_screenshot`

**Data anchor:** None.

**Compliance:** Generalize the property. Never identify a real listing.

---

## Listing photo fails

**Friction:** Toilet-mirror selfie. Agent reflected in oven door. Bedroom photo with a pet on the bed. "Living room" that is clearly a hallway.

**Audience:** Both. Universally funny.

**Best templates:** `expectation_vs_reality`, `pull_quote_card`

**Data anchor:** None.

**Compliance:** The joke is *the photo*, not *the agent who took it*. If a specific agent could identify themselves, kill it. Only safe target is anonymous archetype.

## Inspection drama

**Friction:** Inspector finds a "crack" that's a hair on the lens. Or finds a real $40K septic problem the seller "didn't know about."

**Audience:** Agent-coded with consumer carryover.

**Best templates:** `pov_youre_a`, `this_is_fine_dog`

**Data anchor:** None usually.

**Compliance:** Composites only.

---

## Closing day chaos

**Friction:** Wire fraud alert at 11am. Lender pulling docs at noon. Garage opener missing. The seller forgot to leave the storage unit key.

**Audience:** Both, agent-heavy.

**Best templates:** `this_is_fine_dog`, `nobody_me_at_3am`, `imessage_screenshot`

**Data anchor:** None.

**Compliance:** Composites. Wire fraud joke must not minimize the real risk — frame it as "here is the chaos and here is why we double-verify."

## Commission conversation (post-NAR settlement)

**Friction:** The buyer-agent commission conversation. The "I have to ask the seller to pay my agent" moment. The compliance pamphlet stack.

**Audience:** Agent-heavy. Consumers get the joke if it's framed clearly.

**Best templates:** `pull_quote_card`, `pov_youre_a`, `drake_yes_no`

**Data anchor:** Optional. Could cite Bend buyer-rep agreements signed % or comparable.

**Compliance:** This is the highest-risk category. Stay on agent-self-roasting and the absurdity of the rollout. Do not give legal advice. Do not imply who pays what.

## Bend-specific (the moat)

**Friction options:**
- The Cascades view that's actually a 4-mile-out distant ridge
- Rural acreage with a "septic" that needs $40K replacement
- Transplants pricing out locals who priced out their kids
- Short-term rental zoning surprises
- Wildfire smoke season as an unwritten contingency
- Old Bend / new Bend / Tetherow / NorthWest Crossing tribal humor
- The "I moved from Portland for the small-town feel" + Trader Joe's parking lot
- Drake / Park / Galveston traffic at school pickup

**Audience:** Local-heavy. Lower reach but higher conversion.

**Best templates:** `tell_me_without_telling_me`, `pov_youre_a`, `pull_quote_card`, `expectation_vs_reality`

**Data anchor:** Optional. STR permit counts, transplant migration data, wildfire smoke days/year all available from public sources.

**Compliance:** The transplant joke is the trap door. Stay on the *housing math*, not the *people*. "Transplants priced out locals" is a market observation. "[Group] are ruining Bend" is steering-adjacent and a hard no.

**Why this category gets priority:** No other RE meme account in the country can do Bend humor. This is the defensible moat.

## MLS workflow (agent-only)

**Friction:** The MLS down meme. Required-fields error at 11pm. The "Coming Soon" status confusion. Feature-tag autocomplete.

**Audience:** Agent-only. Consumers will scroll past.

**Best templates:** `nobody_me_at_3am`, `pov_youre_a`, `imessage_screenshot`

**Data anchor:** None.

**Compliance:** Internal-baseball, low risk. Just don't name a specific MLS staffer.

**Use sparingly:** Agent-only memes don't reach the consumer audience that drives leads. Mix in for follower retention with the agent peer set.

## Open house scenarios

**Friction:** Zero attendees. Or: the same neighbor from three open houses ago. Or: the buyer who wants to discuss every comp in the neighborhood.

**Audience:** Both.

**Best templates:** `pov_youre_a`, `imessage_screenshot`, `expectation_vs_reality`

**Data anchor:** None.

**Compliance:** Composites.

## Topics that look fun but are landmines

**Banned outright:**

- "Family-friendly neighborhood" jokes (familial-status steering)
- "Single mom buyer" tropes (sex + familial status)
- "Up-and-coming area" (often coded for demographic change — Fair Housing risk)
- "Diverse community" jokes (race / national origin steering)
- Religious decor in listing photos (religion is protected)
- Accessibility features in listings (disability is protected)
- Section 8 / voucher humor (source of income — protected in Oregon)
- Political content even tangential (rate-cycle math is fine, partisan framing is not)
- A specific real listing where the agent or seller is identifiable
- Specific real client names or identifying details
- AI-generated property photos (Anti-Slop Rule 2)
- Forecasts without "this is opinion + verified data" framing

If a meme idea touches any of the above, route it to a different friction topic. Do not "be careful" with it. Skip it.
