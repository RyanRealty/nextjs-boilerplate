# Distribution — Earnest.

How Earnest. episodes reach an audience, build a community, and quietly serve Ryan Realty's pipeline without breaking the prestige aesthetic.

The architecture is **owned media first, vertical social as funnel only**. Mass social platforms (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) are the discovery layer. The destination is the Earnest. microsite, the Substack newsletter, and the Discord community. This inverts the typical creator-economy default and is the right architecture for a prestige anthology brand. (Reference research: indie serialized creators in 2026 build durable audiences on owned platforms; mass-social audiences are rented from algorithms that change weekly.)

## Four-Tier Architecture

### Tier 1 — Owned (the moat)

- **earnest.show** — standalone microsite. Episode hub pages, season-arc pages, a pull-quote library, a credits crawl page (where the Ryan Realty production credit and AI voice disclosure live). Inter Display + Editorial New, palette per `BRAND_SYSTEM.md`. NO retargeting pixels. NO push-notifications. The site is a destination, not a conversion funnel.
- **earnest.substack.com** — Substack newsletter.
  - Free tier: episode drops, episode pull-quotes, brief writer's notes.
  - Paid tier ($5/month): writer's-room essays, alternate cuts, deleted scenes, character-bible deep dives, behind-the-scenes notes from the AI cinematographer.
  - Realistic Year-1 target: 1,000 paid subscribers = $5,000 MRR.
- **Discord server** for paid Substack subscribers + free fan tier.
  - Monthly live writers'-room sessions (text-only, Q&A about character decisions, episode mechanics).
  - Channel for episode discussion, character speculation, fan theories.
  - Realistic Year-1 target: 5,000 free members + 500 paid.
- **Quarterly print zine** — pull-quote library, behind-the-scenes essays, reader letters. Sent to paid Substack subscribers. Real, tactile, mailed. The artifact reinforces the thematic bridge to Ryan Realty without breaking the prestige aesthetic. (The Welcome to Night Vale model.)

### Tier 2 — Discovery (the funnel)

- **YouTube channel** — full episodes uploaded as long-form Shorts (vertical embedded in landscape canvas) with rigorous SEO discipline. Long-tail keyword targets: "AI short film about [scenario]," "stylized animation about [emotional topic]," etc. Best 2027/2028 long-tail discovery channel.
- **Vimeo** — festival-quality master uploads. Submit to Vimeo Staff Picks for prestige discovery. Letterboxd partnership amplifies.
- **TikTok / Reels / Shorts / Facebook Reels** — episode TRAILERS only (15-30s teaser cuts), NOT full episodes. Caption + comment-keyword CTA points viewers to YouTube and earnest.show for the full episode. Vertical platforms are funnel, not destination.

### Tier 3 — Festivals (legitimization)

- **Runway AI Film Festival (AIFF)** — May submission for fall festival. Single episode entry. Flagship AI cinema festival, largest reach.
- **AI International Film Festival (aifilmfest.org)** — annual, April. 2026 winners include "BUT I WAS DIFFERENT" (Italy, Best Documentary + Best Film), "Eclipse" (Colombia, Best AI Animation Short), "WCNSF" (Spain, Best AI Film). Earnest. fits the Animation Short and Drama Short categories.
- **Tribeca Storyscapes** + **SXSW Episodic Pilot Competition** — submit once a complete season ships. Tribeca's 2026 OpenAI Sora Shorts program signals the festival circuit is opening to AI cinema fast.
- **Frame Forward AI Animated Film Festival** — inaugural 2026, jury includes Google's Neil Parris, David Dinerstein. Animation short category.
- **Sparknify Human vs. AI Festival** — Sept 26, 2026, San Francisco.

A festival prize unlocks the Tier 4 conversation. Until a season is in the can, festivals are the long-tail goal, not the immediate metric.

### Tier 4 — Long-game (the upside option)

After Season 1 ships and one festival prize lands:
- Brief **Apple TV+ Shorts** programming team (the prestige fit).
- Brief **Netflix vertical/short-form initiative** (Netflix acquired InterPositive in March 2026 for ~$600M; AI cinema is on their roadmap).
- Brief **Amazon Prime Video** (vertical short-form is a 2026 priority).
- Brief **HBO Max** (least likely fit but worth the conversation if a prize lands).

No AI-native serialized show has been picked up by a major streamer yet (as of May 2026). The acquisitions to date are tooling, not content. Earnest. as the first prestige AI anthology has first-mover position. The realistic path: build the IP, prove the audience, win one festival, then pitch from leverage.

## The 7-Day Cadence (Season 1 launch)

Season 1 ships across seven consecutive days. Validated against the May 2026 research on serialized vertical content: rapid release beats slow drip; multi-part series get 2.8× return-viewer rate.

```
Day 1 (Mon)  Episode 1: "The Empty Room"
Day 2 (Tue)  Episode 2: "The Garage"
Day 3 (Wed)  Episode 3: "The Counter"
Day 4 (Thu)  Episode 4: "The Walk-Through"
Day 5 (Fri)  Episode 5: "The Closing Table"
Day 6 (Sat)  Episode 6: "The Open House"
Day 7 (Sun)  Episode 7: "The First Showing"
```

Each day:
- 7:00 AM Pacific: full episode goes live on earnest.show + YouTube + Vimeo simultaneously.
- 9:00 AM Pacific: trailer (15-30s teaser cut) goes live on TikTok + IG Reels + YT Shorts + FB Reels.
- 6:00 PM Pacific: Substack newsletter sends to free + paid lists.
- 8:00 PM Pacific: Discord announcement + writers'-room thread opens for that episode.

## Comment-Keyword CTA (vertical platforms only)

For trailers on TikTok / Reels / Shorts / FB, the caption ends with a comment-keyword CTA pointed at a real Ryan Realty asset already in FUB. Per the May 2026 research on vertical-platform 2026 conversion mechanics, comment-keyword CTAs out-convert link-in-bio.

| Episode | Topic | Keyword | Lead magnet |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "The Empty Room" | DOWNSIZE | Empty-nester downsize playbook |
| 2 | "The Garage" | INHERIT | Inheritance disposition guide |
| 3 | "The Counter" | OFFER | Multiple-offer playbook |
| 4 | "The Walk-Through" | INSPECT | Final walk-through checklist |
| 5 | "The Closing Table" | SETTLE | Divorce-divestment guide |
| 6 | "The Open House" | LIST | Seller's open-house playbook |
| 7 | "The First Showing" | RELOCATE | Bend relocation guide |

Mechanics:
- Comment must be the exact keyword. The auto-DM responder in FUB sends the lead magnet as a PDF link.
- Caption format: "Comment {KEYWORD} for the {asset name}. Full episode at earnest.show."
- The lead magnet must already exist in FUB before the episode trailer ships. Verify the day before.
- DM response sets lead source attribution to `EARNEST_S1_E{N}` so we can track conversion by episode.

## FUB Integration

- Each lead magnet PDF is attached to a FUB campaign with the source code `EARNEST_S1_E{N}` or `EARNEST_MICROSITE`.
- After the lead magnet is delivered, a 5-day nurture sequence in FUB:
  - Day 0: Lead magnet delivery + a "if this resonated, here's the long-form essay" link.
  - Day 2: A second related episode link.
  - Day 5: A "would you like to talk to a Ryan Realty agent about your situation" soft offer.
  - Day 9: A market-data drop relevant to the lead's situation (CMA tool, neighborhood report).
  - Day 14: A "we're here when you're ready" sign-off; lead moves to long-term nurture.

Broker assignment happens manually in FUB based on geography and scenario fit. The brand layer earns the trust; the broker layer closes the relationship.

## Discoverability Discipline

- **YouTube SEO 2026** (per the research): caption text is search-indexed. Always include the episode title verbatim, the keyword, and 2-3 search-relevant phrases ("downsizing as grief," "first-time homebuyer anxiety," "inheriting family home").
- **Title field format:** "Earnest. E01: The Empty Room | A short film about downsizing"
- **Description format:** First 150 characters carry the worldview sentence + the keyword. Body includes the full episode treatment + credits crawl + AI voice disclosure.
- **Vimeo:** title + tags optimized for festival-circuit discovery. "AI short film," "claymation drama," "anthology drama."
- **Letterboxd entry** for each episode: tagged anthology, AI cinema, drama, short film.
- **earnest.show SEO:** episode hub pages with full transcripts, pull-quote library, character bibles abridged for public consumption. This is the long-tail compounding asset.

## What NOT to Do

- Do NOT put a "follow for more" CTA in the video. The CTA lives in the trailer caption.
- Do NOT include a Ryan Realty logo in any video frame. The "Earnest." mark is the entire visible brand.
- Do NOT put Matt's name, photo, voice, or persona in any frame, any caption, any bio.
- Do NOT include phone numbers, URLs, or agent names in the video. All conversion happens via earnest.show + comment-keyword + bio link.
- Do NOT cross-promote other Earnest. episodes within an episode. Each episode stands alone.
- Do NOT publish full episodes on TikTok / Reels / Shorts. Trailers only. The full episode is the destination.
- Do NOT publish on a slow-drip schedule. Seven consecutive days, full stop.
- Do NOT put a retargeting pixel on earnest.show. Privacy and prestige first.
- Do NOT auto-DM viewers who don't opt in via comment-keyword. Inbound only.
